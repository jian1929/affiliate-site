Amazon FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) has been the go-to solution for e-commerce sellers for over a decade. But rising fees, storage costs, and service delays have many sellers exploring FBA alternatives.

The good news? The fulfillment landscape has evolved dramatically. Third-party logistics (3PL) providers now offer comparable—or better—service at competitive prices, with greater flexibility.

Market Shift

Over 40% of Amazon FBA sellers are now using at least one alternative fulfillment method alongside or instead of FBA, according to 2026 seller surveys.

Why Sellers Are Moving Away from FBA

Understanding the pain points helps you evaluate alternatives more effectively.

1. Rising FBA Costs

Amazon's FBA fees have increased 30-50% since 2020. The 2026 fee changes included:

  • Fulfillment fees increased by $0.25-$0.50 per unit
  • Storage fees now peak at $2.40/cubic foot during Q4
  • Long-term storage fees now apply after 271 days (reduced from 365)
  • Return processing fees added for apparel and shoes

2. Storage Limits and Restrictions

FBA's Inventory Performance Index (IPI) requirements mean sellers can hit storage limits just before peak season. Alternative providers offer more predictable capacity.

3. Lack of Branding Control

With FBA, your products ship in plain brown boxes with Amazon labeling. Some alternatives allow custom packaging and inserts—critical for brand building.

4. Multi-Channel Complexity

Sellers using Shopify, WooCommerce, Walmart, or their own websites alongside Amazon often struggle with FBA's siloed approach. Multi-channel fulfillment networks solve this.

5. Speed and Reliability Concerns

While FBA still offers Prime shipping, some alternatives now match or exceed FBA speed for non-Amazon orders, with dedicated customer support.

ShipBob

ShipBob has emerged as the leading FBA alternative for direct-to-consumer brands. Their technology-first approach combines warehouse management with fulfillment excellence.

Key Features

  • 30+ fulfillment center locations across US, Canada, UK, EU, Australia
  • 2-day shipping coverage for 100% of US population
  • Custom packaging with your branding
  • Integration with Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Amazon, eBay, Walmart
  • Returns management built-in
  • Same-day dispatch cutoff (2PM local time)
  • Value-added services: kitting, gift wrapping, personalization

Pricing

Pros

  • Excellent technology platform with real-time tracking
  • Custom packaging for brand experience
  • Strong multi-channel integration
  • Fast, reliable 2-day shipping
  • Dedicated customer success manager

Cons

  • Minimum volume requirements for enterprise features
  • Higher base cost than some alternatives
  • You don't control the warehouses

Shopify Fulfillment Network (SFN)

4.3/5 Best for: Shopify-first brands

Shopify Fulfillment Network is Shopify's answer to FBA, leveraging their platform advantage to offer integrated fulfillment for Shopify merchants.

Key Features

  • Strategic warehouse locations for 2-day delivery coverage
  • Deep Shopify integration (one-click sync)
  • Machine learning-powered inventory allocation
  • Automated shipping rules and preferences
  • Unified returns portal
  • Supports Shopify Markets for international

Pricing

Storage: $3.30 per cubic foot per month. Fulfillment: $5.50+ per order. See SFN pricing

Pros

  • Seamless Shopify integration
  • Intelligent inventory placement
  • No additional POS or app costs for Shopify users
  • Backed by Shopify's resources

Cons

  • Limited to Shopify ecosystem (not ideal for multi-channel)
  • Newer service, still scaling
  • Premium pricing for Shopify integration

Sellbrite

4.4/5 Best for: Multi-channel sellers needing inventory control

Sellbrite is a multi-channel inventory and listing management platform that integrates with various 3PL providers, giving you the best of both worlds.

Key Features

  • List and manage products across Amazon, eBay, Walmart, Shopify, BigCommerce
  • Centralized inventory sync with automatic quantity updates
  • Multi-warehouse inventory management
  • Integration with ShipBob, Rakuten, and other 3PLs
  • Repricer and pricing rules
  • Reporting and analytics dashboard

Pricing

Plans from $49/month for Basic (up to 100 SKUs), $199/month for Plus (up to 5,000 SKUs). View pricing

Pros

  • Excellent multi-channel management
  • Works with your existing 3PL or FBA
  • Prevents overselling across channels
  • Flexible—use any fulfillment provider

Cons

  • Requires separate fulfillment arrangement
  • Not a fulfillment provider itself
  • Higher SKUs means higher plan costs

  • Custom packaging available

  • Good for sellers moving beyond Amazon

  • Limited Shopify-native integration compared to SFN
  • Less international coverage
  • Smaller network than ShipBob

Third-Party Logistics (3PL/蕾波 Warehousing)

4.5/5 Best for: Maximum flexibility and control

Traditional 3PL providers offer the most customization. You ship inventory to their warehouse, they store and ship on your behalf.

How It Works

  1. Ship bulk inventory to 3PL warehouse
  2. 3PL receives, counts, and stores your goods
  3. Orders come through your system (Shopify, WooCommerce, etc.)
  4. 3PL picks, packs, and ships orders
  5. Inventory updates sync back to your system

Top 3PL Providers

  • 蕾波.com: Network of warehouses, good for growing brands
  • Ware2Go: UPS-backed, strong reliability
  • Red Stag Fulfillment: Specializes in oversize/fragile items
  • ShipMonk: Tech-forward, good for e-commerce

Pricing

Storage: $6-12 per pallet per month. Pick & pack: $2-4 per order. Varies significantly by provider and location.

Pros

  • Maximum flexibility in operations
  • Negotiable pricing based on volume
  • Custom packaging and branding
  • No long-term commitments (usually)
  • Can work with any sales channel

Cons

  • Requires more management than integrated solutions
  • Quality varies by provider
  • Integration complexity
  • You're responsible for choosing a reliable partner

Self-Fulfillment

3.5/5 Best for: Very small volume or test products

Self-fulfillment means you handle storage and shipping from home or a rented space. Not scalable long-term, but viable for certain situations.

When Self-Fulfillment Makes Sense

  • Testing new products before committing to volume
  • Highly customized or handmade products
  • Very low order volumes (<50/month)
  • Products requiring special handling

Practical Considerations

  • Storage: Dedicate a room or garage to inventory
  • Supplies: Buy boxes, tape, bubble wrap in bulk
  • Shipping: Negotiate carrier accounts (Stamps.com, Pirate Ship)
  • Time: Budget 15-30 minutes per order

Pros

  • No per-order fees
  • Complete control over packaging
  • No minimum volumes
  • Maximum profit margins per order

Cons

  • Doesn't scale
  • No 2-day shipping guarantee
  • Time-intensive
  • Personal space required
  • Stressful during peak seasons

When to Use Each FBA Alternative

You're a Shopify Brand

Use: Shopify Fulfillment Network

The native integration eliminates friction. If Shopify is your primary store, SFN's machine-learning-powered placement can actually beat FBA for your customers.

You Sell Across Multiple Platforms

Use: ShipBob

ShipBob's universal integrations mean you can sell on Amazon, Shopify, Walmart, eBay, and your own site—all fulfilled from the same inventory.

You Need Maximum Control

Use: Traditional 3PL

For custom packaging, special handling, or negotiated rates, work directly with a 3PL provider. You trade simplicity for flexibility.

You're Just Starting Out

Use: Self-Fulfill or Dropshipping

Keep costs low until you understand your sales velocity. Move to a fulfillment partner once you're consistently shipping 20+ orders per week.

You Want to Reduce FBA Dependence

Use: Hybrid Approach

Many successful sellers use FBA for Prime-eligible products while fulfilling non-Prime orders through ShipBob or another 3PL. This hedges against FBA disruptions.

You Have Fragile or Oversized Products

Use: Red Stag Fulfillment

Red Stag specializes in items FBA struggles with—furniture, electronics, items over 150 lbs. They have the expertise and insurance.

Ready to Diversify Your Fulfillment?

FBA isn't going away, but it no longer has to be your only option—or even your primary one. The right fulfillment strategy can reduce costs, improve customer experience, and give you flexibility.
