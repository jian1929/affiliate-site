What is the A9 Algorithm?

A9 (now more accurately called the Amazon search algorithm) is the system that determines which products appear in Amazon search results and in what order. Every time a customer types a query, A9 evaluates millions of products in milliseconds to deliver the most relevant and potentially convertable results.

Understanding A9 isn't about "gaming" the system—it's about understanding what Amazon rewards and aligning your listing optimization accordingly. The algorithm's goal is simple: maximize revenue per search. Amazon earns when you sell, so it ranks products most likely to convert.

💡 Key Insight: Amazon's algorithm is not trying to show you the "best" product—it's trying to show you the product most likely to result in a sale. Conversion is king.

How A9 Actually Works

A9 operates in two phases for every search query:

Phase 1: Matching

When you search "laptop stand aluminum", A9 identifies all products whose titles, bullets, and backend keywords contain those terms. This narrows millions down to thousands of potentially relevant products.

Phase 2: Ranking

The matching products are then ranked using a combination of conversion metrics, relevancy scores, and customer satisfaction signals. The top results appear on page 1, where 70% of clicks occur.

The Ranking Score Formula

While Amazon doesn't publish exact formulas, sellers and analysts have reverse-engineered the key components:

Sales Velocity Highest Impact

Units sold in a given time period. A product selling 100 units in 7 days ranks higher than one selling 100 units in 30 days. Velocity matters more than total volume.

Conversion Rate Very High Impact

Percentage of visitors who buy. If 10% of visitors to your listing purchase, versus 3% for competitors, A9 rewards you with better placement.

Relevancy Score High Impact

How well your listing matches the search query. Keywords in titles and bullets signal stronger relevance than backend-only keywords.

Customer Reviews & Ratings Medium Impact

Average star rating and total review count. New listings can overcome low reviews with strong conversion rates.

Price Medium Impact

Price affects conversion and is factored into ranking. Very low prices can signal low quality and hurt conversion.

The 5 Core Ranking Factors

Based on years of testing and analysis, here are the five factors that most significantly impact Amazon rankings:

1. Sales Performance (Conversion Rate × Velocity)

Amazon tracks three conversion metrics:

  • Offer Conversion Rate (OCR) — Clicks to buy percentage
  • Listing Conversion Rate (LCR) — Sessions to units ordered
  • Branded Search Conversion — Search for your brand, then purchase

Products with higher conversion rates get boosted placements, creating a flywheel effect: better placement → more visibility → more sales → better placement.

2. Keyword Relevance

A9 parses your listing content to match search queries:

  • Title keywords — Highest weight (front-loaded terms most important)
  • Bullet point keywords — High weight
  • Backend search terms — Hidden but indexed
  • Description — Lowest but still indexed

3. Stock & Availability

Products must be in stock to rank. Additionally:

  • FBA listings often rank higher due to Prime eligibility
  • Products with recent stockouts may see ranking drops
  • Overstocking can indicate poor demand, hurting rankings

4. Price Performance

Price affects rankings through multiple mechanisms:

  • Optimal price range varies by category
  • Extremely low prices can signal low quality
  • Price changes can trigger ranking volatility
  • Buy Box wins correlate with better rankings

5. Listing Quality

Amazon evaluates listing completeness and quality:

  • Complete content (all 5 bullets, A+ content)
  • High-quality images (especially main image compliance)
  • Keyword optimization without stuffing
  • Grammar and spelling correctness

Conversion Optimization

Since conversion rate is the highest-impact factor you can control, let's break down how to maximize it:

Click-Through Rate (CTR) Optimization

Before you can convert, customers must click. CTR from search results depends on:

  • Main Image Quality — White background, professional lighting, product fills 85%
  • Title Clarity — Buyers scan, so front-load essential information
  • Star Rating — Low ratings repel clicks; 4.3+ is the sweet spot
  • Price — Visible price must seem fair relative to alternatives
  • FBA Badge — Prime eligibility signals reliability

Landing Page Conversion

Once clicked, your listing must close the sale:

  • Images — Answer questions before buyers ask them
  • Bullets — Focus on benefits, not just features
  • Reviews — Address negative review themes in your content
  • Price — Anchor against MSRP or competitors when applicable

Relevance Signals

Amazon determines relevance through text matching and behavioral signals:

Text-Based Relevance

A9 extracts keywords from your listing to match search queries:

  • Exact matches — "laptop stand" matches "laptop stand"
  • Stem matches — "running" matches "runs"
  • Synonym matches — "laptop stand" matches "notebook holder"
  • Compound matches — "laptop stand aluminum" matches "aluminum laptop riser"

Behavioral Relevance

What customers do after searching signals relevance:

  • Click-through rate — Do searchers click your listing?
  • Session duration — Do they linger (read content) or bounce immediately?
  • Add-to-cart rate — Are they considering purchase?
  • Purchase rate — Did they actually buy?

Category and Browse Node Relevance

Where you list your product affects visibility:

  • Primary category determines browse node placement
  • Secondary categories expand reach but dilute focus
  • Classification accuracy affects both search and recommendations

Debunking Common Myths

❌ MYTH: Amazon punishes you for raising prices.

TRUTH: Amazon doesn't directly penalize higher prices. However, higher prices typically reduce conversion rates, which hurts rankings indirectly. The algorithm rewards sales, not low prices.

❌ MYTH: Running out of stock kills your rankings forever.

TRUTH: Temporary stockouts cause temporary ranking drops. Once restocked, rankings often recover. Extended outages (weeks+) cause longer-term damage as competitors capture the position.

❌ MYTH: More keywords = better rankings.

TRUTH: Keyword stuffing hurts conversion by making listings unreadable. Focus on relevant keywords that match what buyers actually search for.

❌ MYTH: Amazon PPC doesn't help organic rankings.

TRUTH: PPC can indirectly help organic rankings by driving sales velocity and conversion signals. A product that sells well with ads tends to sell well organically.

❌ MYTH: New listings can't compete with established ones.

TRUTH: New listings with strong conversion rates can rank quickly. Speed of ranking depends more on sales velocity than age or review count.
