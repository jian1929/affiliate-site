Why Understanding FBA Fees Matters

Every successful Amazon seller knows one thing: your profit isn't your selling price minus your product cost. It's your selling price minus ALL fees—including Amazon's.

New sellers often launch products only to discover that Amazon's fees eat their margins. By understanding fees upfront, you can:

  • Price products correctly from day one
  • Choose products with healthy margins
  • Avoid the trap of unprofitable inventory
  • Build a sustainable business model

The Golden Rule of Amazon Pricing

Your product cost should be 25-35% of your selling price. This leaves room for FBA fees, PPC, returns, and still maintains a healthy profit margin.

The Holiday Storage Trap

October-December storage fees are 3x higher. If you stock up for Q4, calculate whether you'll sell through before January—or face $2.40/cubic foot monthly charges.

4. Long-Term Storage Fees

If inventory sits in Amazon's warehouse for over 365 days, you're charged additional fees:

  • $6.90 per cubic foot (on the 15th of each month)
  • Plus $0.15 per unit for items with processing time over 165 days

Avoid Long-Term Storage Fees

  • Set up automated inventory alerts at 180 days
  • Create removal orders before 365 days
  • Use FBA Liquidations to recover some value
  • Consider advertising to move stagnant inventory

5. Return Processing Fees

When customers return items, Amazon charges a return processing fee for categories with high return rates:

  • Apparel
  • Shoes
  • Jewelry
  • Luggage
  • Watches

The fee is approximately 50-70% of the fulfillment fee for the item.

How to Reduce FBA Fees

1. Optimize Product Weight and Size

Every ounce counts. Work with your manufacturer to reduce packaging size. A product that drops from 2 lbs to 1 lb saves $0.85 per unit in fulfillment fees alone.

2. Choose Products in Lower-Fee Categories

Beauty products at 8% vs. Jewelry at 20% is a 12% difference on every sale. Factor fees into product selection.

3. Use FBA Calculator Before Launching

Amazon's FBA Calculator (in Seller Central) shows exact fees for any product. Use it during product research—not after.

4. Keep Inventory Moving

Fast-moving inventory spreads storage fees across more units. Use PPC and promotions to move slow sellers before storage fees accumulate.

5. Consider FBM for Oversize Items

Large, heavy items have expensive FBA fees. If your product is bulky, compare FBA vs. FBM costs using the FBA Calculator.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Amazon charge fees if I don't sell anything?

Only the $39.99 monthly subscription (if you have a Professional account). Individual sellers only pay $0.99 per item when they sell. Storage fees only apply if you have inventory in FBA warehouses.

Are FBA fees the same for all sellers?

Yes, FBA fees are standardized. However, if you're in the FBA New Selection program or have certain agreements with Amazon, you may get temporary fee reductions.

What's the average FBA fee percentage?

For most categories, total FBA fees (referral + fulfillment + storage) typically run 25-40% of the selling price. This is why product cost should be 25-35% of price for healthy margins.

Do FBA fees include shipping to the customer?

Yes! That's the value of FBA. The fulfillment fee covers picking, packing, and shipping. Prime customers get free 2-day shipping, but Amazon covers that cost.

Can I negotiate FBA fees?

Not directly. However, high-volume sellers (millions in revenue) can work with Amazon's account management for custom arrangements. For most sellers, fees are fixed.

What happens to fees on returned items?

If a customer returns an item within 30 days and you receive it back in sellable condition, the fulfillment fee is refunded. However, return processing fees (for applicable categories) are not refunded.
