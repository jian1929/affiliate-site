Key Features to Look For

Regardless of which tool you choose, make sure it includes these essential features:

1. Demand Forecasting

The software should analyze your sales history and predict future demand. This helps you order the right quantity, not just enough to barely avoid stockouts.

2. Reorder Point Alerts

Get notified before you run out. The best tools consider lead time from your supplier so you're always ordering at the optimal moment.

3. Lead Time Tracking

Know how long it takes from PO creation to inventory on shelves. This is crucial for seasonal products and overseas suppliers.

4. Storage Fee Calculator

Amazon's storage fees vary by month. Good software helps you avoid long-term storage fees by reminding you to move aged inventory.

5. Multi-Channel Integration

If you sell on multiple platforms (Shopify, Walmart, eBay), your inventory management must sync across all channels to prevent overselling.

Common Inventory Management Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls that even experienced sellers make:

  1. Setting reorder points too low: Always add buffer for unexpected demand spikes and shipping delays
  2. Ignoring seasonality: Your reorder points should adjust for Q4 and other peak seasons
  3. Not tracking lead times: Ordering when you run out is too late — order when you place the PO
  4. Over-relying on FBA stock alerts: Amazon's alerts are often delayed — use third-party monitoring
  5. Forgetting about dead stock: Track aged inventory and bundle or liquidate before storage fees eat profits

FAQ: Amazon Inventory Management

Do I really need inventory management software?

If you have more than 20 SKUs or over $10K in monthly revenue, yes. Manual tracking becomes error-prone and time-consuming. The cost of a stockout (lost sales, ranking drops, emergency shipping) far exceeds the software subscription.

Can I use Excel for Amazon inventory management?

You can, but it's not recommended. Excel works for very small operations, but lacks real-time sync, automated alerts, and demand forecasting. As your catalog grows, Excel becomes a liability.

How often should I check inventory levels?

With good software, daily is sufficient. Set alerts for critical items (under 2 weeks of stock) and review weekly reports for demand trends and reorder planning.

What's the reorder point formula?

Basic formula: Reorder Point = (Average Daily Sales × Lead Time) + Safety Stock

For example: 10 units/day × 14-day lead time + 30-unit safety stock = 170 units. Order when stock hits 170.
