Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best graphic design software for beginners?

Canva is the clear winner for beginners. Its drag-and-drop interface, thousands of templates, and helpful tutorials make it easy to create professional designs without any design experience.

Is Canva Pro worth it over the free version?

Yes, if you create content regularly. Canva Pro includes 420,000+ templates, unlimited brand kits, Magic Resize, background remover, and 100GB cloud storage. The time savings alone justify the cost.

Can Figma replace Photoshop?

Partially. Figma excels at UI/UX design and prototyping but isn't designed for photo editing. For photo manipulation and raster graphics, you still need Photoshop or Affinity Photo.

Is Affinity Designer a good alternative to Illustrator?

For many users, yes. Affinity Designer handles most vector tasks excellently and costs a fraction of Illustrator's annual subscription. However, for print production, complex typography, or team workflows, Illustrator remains the standard.

What's the best option for social media design?

Canva dominates social media design with platform-specific templates, Magic Resize for multi-platform posting, and scheduled publishing integration. It's the fastest way to maintain a consistent social presence.

Should I get Adobe CC or Canva?

Choose Adobe CC if you need professional-grade tools, print design, or plan to work with agencies. Choose Canva if you need speed, simplicity, and don't want to invest time in learning complex software.

What's the best design software for iPad?

Procreate is the top choice for digital illustration on iPad ($13 one-time). For general design, Canva works great on iPad. For professional vector work, Affinity Designer on iPad is surprisingly capable.
