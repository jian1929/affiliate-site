Pricing Breakdown: Shopify vs WooCommerce vs BigCommerce

Understanding the total cost of ownership is essential for budgeting your e-commerce venture.

Shopify Pricing

Shopify offers tiered monthly plans with all hosting included:

  • Starter: $5/month - Limited features, not recommended
  • Basic Shopify: $29/month - Basic features, 2 accounts, 4 staff
  • Shopify: $79/month - Professional reports, 5 staff accounts
  • Advanced Shopify: $299/month - Third-party calculated shipping, 15 staff
  • Shopify Plus: $2,000+/month - Enterprise features

Additional costs: Shopify Payments eliminates transaction fees; otherwise 0.5-2% per transaction.

WooCommerce Pricing

WooCommerce is free as a plugin, but requires hosting and extras:

  • WooCommerce plugin: Free
  • Hosting: $10-$100+/month (managed WooCommerce recommended)
  • Domain: $10-$20/year
  • SSL Certificate: $0-$200/year (often free with hosting)
  • Premium themes: $0-$200 (one-time)
  • Premium plugins: $0-$500+/year
  • Maintenance: $0-$100/month (DIY vs managed)

True cost: $20-$200+/month total, but no platform transaction fees.

BigCommerce Pricing

BigCommerce offers transparent pricing with no transaction fees:

  • Standard: $29/month - Up to $50,000 revenue
  • Plus: $71/month - Up to $180,000 revenue
  • Pro: $134/month - Up to $1,000,000 revenue
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing - Above $1M revenue

Key advantage: No transaction fees on any plan, just the payment processor fees.

Features Comparison: Shopify vs WooCommerce vs BigCommerce

Shopify Features

Shopify provides a complete, polished e-commerce experience:

  • Shopify POS: Unified in-person and online sales
  • Shopify Payments: Built-in payment processing (Stripe-powered)
  • Shopify Fulfillment Network: Warehousing and shipping
  • Shopify Shipping: Discounted shipping labels
  • Shopify Admin: Mobile app for store management
  • Multi-currency: Automatic currency conversion
  • Abandoned cart recovery: Automatic email reminders
  • Product reviews: Built-in review system
  • Discount codes: Flexible promotional options
  • Blog integration: Content marketing support

WooCommerce Features

WooCommerce offers unmatched extensibility through WordPress:

  • WooCommerce core: Free, open-source plugin
  • WooCommerce Payments: Built-in Stripe payments
  • WooCommerce Shipping: Live rates and label printing
  • Subscriptions: Recurring payment support
  • Bookings: Appointment scheduling
  • Memberships: B2B and membership sites
  • Membership: Loyalty program tools
  • Product vendors: Multi-vendor marketplace
  • Dynamic pricing: Advanced pricing rules
  • Photography: Additional image uploads per product

BigCommerce Features

BigCommerce provides enterprise-grade features at reasonable prices:

  • Headless commerce: API-first architecture
  • B2B edition: Dedicated wholesale features
  • Multi-channel: Native Amazon, eBay, Facebook, Instagram
  • Single Page Checkout: Higher conversion rates
  • Customer groups: Tiered pricing for segments
  • Product filtering: Advanced faceted search
  • Related products: AI-powered recommendations
  • Google Customer Reviews: Integration included
  • Abandoned cartSaver: Automatic recovery emails
  • Live shipping rates: Real-time carrier rates

Our Verdict: Which E-commerce Platform Wins?

Best for Beginners: Shopify

If you're starting out and want the easiest path to a professional online store, Shopify is our recommendation. The all-in-one solution handles hosting, security, and payments. You can launch in hours with no technical knowledge. The trade-off is higher monthly costs and some lock-in.

Best for Flexibility: WooCommerce

If you need maximum control, have technical skills, or already use WordPress, WooCommerce is the winner. The flexibility is unmatched, and long-term costs can be lower. However, be prepared to manage your own hosting and security.

Best for Growing B2B: BigCommerce

If you're scaling a B2B business or need native multi-channel selling, BigCommerce is excellent. The no-transaction-fee model and B2B features make it ideal for growing businesses ready to move beyond entry-level platforms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I switch platforms later?

Yes, but it's complex. Data export/import is possible but designs won't transfer. Plan for rebuilding your store visually. Some services specialize in migrations.

Which platform has the lowest total cost?

WooCommerce has the lowest platform cost (free plugin) but requires hosting ($10-$50/month). Shopify starts at $29/month but includes hosting. Calculate total costs including themes and apps.

Which is best for SEO?

All three can achieve excellent SEO. WooCommerce with WordPress SEO plugins offers the most control. BigCommerce has strong built-in SEO. Shopify provides good basics.

Do I need coding skills?

Shopify: No. WooCommerce: Basic for customization. BigCommerce: Basic to moderate. All platforms work without coding.

Which handles high volume best?

Shopify Plus handles enterprise volume. WooCommerce can scale with managed hosting. BigCommerce's Open SaaS architecture scales well without platform changes.

Conclusion

Your e-commerce platform choice depends on your priorities:

  • Ease of use: Choose Shopify
  • Maximum control: Choose WooCommerce
  • B2B and scaling: Choose BigCommerce
  • Budget constraints: WooCommerce (with managed hosting)
  • WordPress integration: WooCommerce
  • POS integration: Shopify

Remember: all three platforms can build successful stores. Choose based on where you are now and where you want to grow. The best platform is the one you'll stick with and optimize over time.
