Amazon generates a treasure trove of data about your business—sales velocity, keyword performance, advertising metrics, inventory movements, and customer behavior. But data without analysis is just noise. This guide teaches you how to transform raw numbers into actionable insights.

Why Sales Data Analysis Matters

Top Amazon sellers share one trait: they're obsessive about data. Not because they love spreadsheets, but because data reveals truth:

  • Which products are actually profitable (not just which sell the most)
  • Where your money is leaking (fees, returns, advertising waste)
  • What your customers really want (beyond what they say)
  • Where the opportunities are (before competitors find them)
  • How to allocate resources (for maximum ROI)

The Data-Driven Seller Advantage

Sellers who analyze their data consistently outperform those who don't. A 5% improvement in conversion rate, a 10% reduction in wasted ad spend, or identifying one underperforming product to discontinue—these small wins compound into massive profit differences.

The Essential Amazon Metrics

Revenue Metrics

Total Revenue

Your gross sales before any deductions. Track daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly.

Don't stop here: Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity.

Net Revenue

Total revenue minus returns, refunds, and chargebacks. This is your real income.

Average Order Value (AOV)

Average revenue per order. Higher AOV typically means better efficiency.

Total Revenue ÷ Number of Orders

Profitability Metrics

Gross Profit Margin

Revenue minus cost of goods sold (COGS), before other expenses.

((Revenue - COGS) ÷ Revenue) × 100

Target: 30%+ for healthy margins

Net Profit Margin

The ultimate measure: what actually makes it to your pocket.

((Revenue - All Costs) ÷ Revenue) × 100

Target: 15%+ for sustainable business

Full Fulfilment Cost (FFC)

All costs per unit including: product cost, shipping, prep, storage, Amazon fees, advertising, and returns.

Unit Economics

Units Sold

Total quantity sold. Track trends over time.

Units Returned

Returns as a percentage of units sold. High = problem.

Return Rate

Returns ÷ Units Sold × 100. Above 5% is concerning.

Refund Rate

Refunds as percentage of revenue. Indicates satisfaction.

Amazon Fee Analysis

Amazon's fees are complex. Break them down to understand your true margins:

  • Referral Fees: 6-15% depending on category
  • FBA Fulfillment Fees: Based on size and weight
  • Monthly Storage Fees: $0.75-$2.40 per cubic foot
  • Long-Term Storage Fees: $6.90-$15.60 per cubic foot after 365 days
  • Closing Fees: $1.80 per unit for media, $0.99 per unit for non-media
  • Refurbishment Fees: $5.50-$13.35 for returned items

Advertising Metrics

Campaign Performance Metrics

ACoS

Advertising Cost of Sales

Ad Spend ÷ Ad Revenue

Lower is better. Target varies by category.

TACoS

Total Advertising Cost of Sales

Ad Spend ÷ Total Revenue

Includes organic sales. Lower is better.

RoAS

Return on Ad Spend

Ad Revenue ÷ Ad Spend

Higher is better. 3x+ is typically good.

CPC

Cost Per Click

What you pay each time someone clicks your ad.

Conversion Metrics

CTR

Click-Through Rate

Clicks ÷ Impressions

Target: 0.5%+ for search ads

CVR

Conversion Rate

Orders ÷ Clicks

Target: 10%+ for optimized listings

Where to Find Amazon Sales Data

Seller Central Reports

  • Business Reports: Sales dashboard, traffic, conversion by ASIN
  • Inventory Reports: Stock levels, age, sell-through rates
  • Payments Reports: Detailed transaction history
  • Advertising Reports: Campaign performance data
  • Amazon Brand Analytics: Search terms, demographics (Brand Registry required)

Building Your Analytics Routine

Daily Review (5 minutes)

  • Units sold yesterday vs. average
  • Any unusual activity (spikes or drops)
  • Customer service messages requiring attention

Weekly Analysis (30 minutes)

  • Sales trends by product
  • Advertising performance (ACoS, RoAS, spend)
  • Inventory levels and reorder needs
  • Competitor activity
  • Review and negative feedback

Monthly Deep Dive (2-3 hours)

  • Profit and loss by product
  • Advertising ROI analysis
  • Return and refund trends
  • Keyword ranking changes
  • Competitive positioning
  • Inventory turnover analysis
  • Strategic adjustments for next month

Common Analysis Mistakes to Avoid

  • Focusing on revenue, not profit: High revenue with low margins is a warning sign
  • Ignoring hidden costs: Include storage, returns, and time costs
  • Not tracking by product: Some products subsidize others
  • Short-term thinking: Look at trends over weeks and months
  • Analysis paralysis: Make decisions and iterate

Building a Dashboard

Create a single view of your most important metrics. Include:

  1. Today's sales (units and revenue)
  2. Week-to-date vs. last week
  3. Month-to-date vs. target
  4. Top 5 products by revenue
  5. ACoS vs. target
  6. Units in stock
  7. Open orders needing attention

Making Data-Driven Decisions

Analysis without action is worthless. Here's how to use your data:

Product Decisions

  • Expand products with high margins and growing sales
  • Optimize products with decent margins but poor conversion
  • Consider discontinuing products with thin or negative margins

Advertising Decisions

  • Increase budgets on campaigns with strong RoAS
  • Reduce or pause campaigns with poor performance
  • Adjust keywords based on conversion data
  • Test new targeting based on what works

Inventory Decisions

  • Pre-order inventory based on sales velocity trends
  • Clear slow movers before storage fees increase
  • Optimize reorder points based on actual demand data

Analytics Checklist

  • Calculate true profit margins (include all costs)
  • Track metrics by product, not just total
  • Set up daily/weekly/monthly review routines
  • Monitor advertising metrics closely
  • Track return rates and root causes
  • Analyze seasonal patterns
  • Use third-party tools for deeper insights
  • Build a dashboard for quick status checks
  • Make decisions based on data, not intuition
  • Review and iterate monthly

Conclusion

Data analysis isn't optional for serious Amazon sellers—it's essential. The insights you gain from tracking and analyzing your metrics reveal opportunities, identify problems, and guide every decision you make.

Start with the basics: know your true profit margins by product, understand your advertising efficiency, and track trends over time. As you get more comfortable, dive deeper into customer behavior, keyword performance, and competitive positioning.

Remember: The goal isn't to collect data—it's to act on insights. Analyze, decide, implement, and repeat. That's how winners are made on Amazon.
