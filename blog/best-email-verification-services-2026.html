How Email Verification Works

Understanding the verification process helps you choose the right service:

  1. Syntax validation - Checks email format (user@domain.com)
  2. Domain validation - Verifies MX records exist
  3. SMTP verification - Connects to mail server to confirm mailbox
  4. Catch-all detection - Identifies servers accepting all emails
  5. Toxic email detection - Flags spam traps and abuse addresses
  6. Role detection - Identifies non-personal addresses
  7. Final classification - Valid, invalid, catch-all, or unknown

Frequently Asked Questions

What is email verification?

Email verification is the process of validating that an email address exists and can receive messages. It checks syntax, domain validity, mailbox existence, and identifies risky addresses like spam traps.

Why is email verification important?

Email verification reduces bounce rates, protects sender reputation, improves deliverability, and saves money by preventing wasted sends to invalid addresses.

How often should I verify my email list?

For active lists, verify quarterly. For cold lists or lists that haven't been verified in 6+ months, verify before any major campaign send.

What's the difference between validation and verification?

Email validation checks format and syntax. Email verification actually connects to the mail server to confirm the mailbox exists and can receive messages.
