Quick Overview

Basecamp is a project management and team communication tool that takes a radically different approach: instead of overwhelming you with features, it provides exactly what most teams need to get work done. Created by 37signals (now Basecamp), the same company behind Ruby on Rails, Basecamp has been simplifying remote team work since 2004. Its signature Hill Charts give teams a unique way to track project progress that focuses on the work itself rather than arbitrary percentages.

Bottom Line: Basecamp is perfect for teams that want a simple, all-in-one solution without the complexity of feature-heavy alternatives. Its flat pricing model and thoughtful design make it a favorite among agencies, freelancers, and remote-first companies who value clarity over complexity.

Key Features

Everything in One Place

Basecamp integrates six essential tools:

  • Message Boards: Company-wide announcements and discussions
  • Campfires: Real-time group chat rooms (like virtual water cooler)
  • To-dos: Task lists with assignments and due dates
  • Schedule: Shared calendar with milestones
  • Docs & Files: Collaborative documents and file storage
  • Automatic Check-ins: Scheduled prompts for team updates

Project-Based Organization

Basecamp structures everything around projects:

  • Each project has its own message board, campfire, to-do lists, schedule, docs, and files
  • Create as many projects as needed
  • Invite clients, contractors, or team members to specific projects
  • Archive completed projects to keep things tidy

Automatic Check-ins

One of Basecamp's most innovative features:

  • Schedule recurring questions for team members
  • Examples: "What did you do today?" or "Any blockers?"
  • Responses appear in a central activity feed
  • Eliminates status meetings
  • Creates a searchable record of progress

Card Table

For teams that prefer Kanban-style boards:

  • Drag-and-drop task organization
  • Visual workflow management
  • Easy status updates

Hill Charts: A Better Way to Track Progress

Basecamp's signature feature, the Hill Chart, reimagines how we think about project progress.

How Hill Charts Work

Instead of arbitrary percentage completion:

  • Tasks are placed on a hill from "Left Edge" to "Right Edge"
  • Left Edge: Work has been started but is still "in the air"
  • Middle: Work is in the tricky middle ground
  • Right Edge: Work is done and shipped

Why This Matters

Traditional percentage completion is misleading:

  • A task 90% done might be stuck in review
  • Easy tasks get marked 100% while hard ones linger
  • People fill in numbers to make themselves look busy

Hill Charts show the actual state of work, not an artificial number.

Quote: "If something is 80% done, is the remaining 20% easy or hard? You can't tell from a percentage. With Hill Charts, you can see where the work actually is on the hill." — Basecamp

Final Verdict

Is Basecamp Right for You?

Basecamp takes a bold stance: simplicity is a feature. For teams overwhelmed by the complexity of tools like Asana, Monday, or Jira, Basecamp offers a breath of fresh air. It's not trying to do everything—it's trying to do what matters, really well.

Choose Basecamp if:

  • You want a simple, no-frills project management tool
  • You're tired of switching between multiple apps
  • You value communication over task tracking
  • Your team struggles with feature overload
  • You're an agency managing multiple client projects
  • You want to reduce meetings with automatic check-ins
  • You appreciate thoughtful, well-designed software

Consider alternatives if:

  • You need advanced project management features (use Monday, Asana, or Jira)
  • You require detailed time tracking
  • You need extensive integrations with other tools
  • You're a large enterprise with complex workflows
  • You need Gantt charts or resource management
  • Advanced reporting is essential
