Frequently Asked Questions

What is wiki software used for?

Wiki software creates collaborative knowledge bases where teams can store, organize, and share documentation, SOPs, meeting notes, and institutional knowledge.

Should I use cloud or self-hosted wiki?

Cloud wikis (Notion, Confluence) offer ease of use and automatic updates. Self-hosted options (BookStack, Wiki.js) give you full control and data ownership but require server management.

What's the difference between a wiki and a knowledge base?

A wiki is typically more open and collaborative. A knowledge base often has more structured content with categories, search, and often includes customer-facing documentation.

How much does enterprise wiki software cost?

Enterprise solutions like Confluence start around $4.50/user/month. Notion's Team plan is $8/user/month. Self-hosted options are free but require infrastructure costs.

Can I migrate from one wiki to another?

Most wikis offer export/import functionality. Notion, Confluence, and Coda all support various export formats. Migration tools exist for common conversions.
