What Is Segment?

Segment, now part of Twilio, is the leading Customer Data Platform (CDP) that helps businesses unify customer data from every touchpoint into a single source of truth. Founded in 2011, Segment was acquired by Twilio in 2020 for $3.2 billion, cementing its position as the industry standard for customer data infrastructure.

At its core, Segment solves a fundamental problem: most companies have customer data scattered across dozens of tools and platforms. Your CRM has contact information, your analytics tool has behavioral data, your support platform has conversation history, and your e-commerce system has purchase records. Segment connects all these sources and creates a unified customer profile.

Why Businesses Choose Segment

Segment acts as the "central nervous system" of your customer data. Instead of spending engineering resources building custom integrations between every tool, Segment provides a unified API layer. Add a new marketing tool? Connect it to Segment in minutes, not months. This approach has made Segment the trusted choice for over 20,000 companies, from startups to Fortune 500 enterprises.

Segment Products Deep Dive

1. Segment Sources — Data Collection Made Simple

Segment provides multiple ways to collect data from your customer touchpoints:

  • Analytics.js - Website tracking via JavaScript snippet or tag manager integration
  • SDKs - Native iOS, Android, React Native, and Flutter SDKs for mobile apps
  • Server SDKs - Node.js, Python, Ruby, Go, Java, .NET, PHP for backend tracking
  • Cloud Sources - Pull data from Salesforce, Stripe, Zendesk, and 100+ SaaS tools
  • Reverse ETL - Push enriched data back to your operational tools

2. Segment Destinations — Send Data Everywhere

Once data is in Segment, route it anywhere:

  • Analytics tools - Google Analytics 4, Amplitude, Mixpanel, Heap
  • Marketing automation - HubSpot, Marketo, Mailchimp, Klaviyo
  • Advertising platforms - Google Ads, Facebook Ads, TikTok, LinkedIn
  • Customer support - Zendesk, Intercom, Freshdesk
  • Data warehouses - Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift, Databricks

3. Segment Profiles — The Single Source of Truth

Segment Profiles creates a 360-degree view of each customer:

  • Merges data from all connected sources automatically
  • Identity graph resolves cross-device and cross-channel behavior
  • Computed traits and audiences update in real-time
  • Historical data available for retroactive analysis
  • Privacy controls built-in for GDPR and CCPA compliance

4. Segment Engage — Activation Without Aggregation

Segment's built-in activation platform lets you act on your unified data:

  • Build audiences based on any combination of traits and behaviors
  • Personalized email and SMS campaigns without leaving Segment
  • A/B testing and journey orchestration
  • Sync audiences to 200+ destinations for targeted activation

MTU-Based Pricing

Segment pricing is based on Monthly Tracked Users (MTUs). An MTU is a unique user who performs at least one event through Segment in a calendar month. This model aligns cost with usage and is more predictable than event-based pricing. Enterprise plans offer custom MTU tiers and volume discounts.

Segment vs The Competition

Segment vs mParticle

Winner: Depends on your use case

  • Segment = More integrations, easier setup, better for general audiences
  • mParticle = Stronger enterprise features, better for complex data governance
  • Both offer similar core CDP functionality

Segment wins on simplicity and ecosystem breadth. mParticle may have an edge for large enterprises with complex compliance requirements.

Segment vs Amplitude CDP

Winner: Segment for data infrastructure, Amplitude for analytics

  • Segment = Data collection and activation platform, tool-agnostic
  • Amplitude CDP = Built on top of analytics, better for product-led growth
  • Segment has more destinations and cloud source integrations

Choose Segment if you need to unify data across many tools. Choose Amplitude CDP if your primary use case is product analytics and you want native activation.

Segment vs RudderStack

Winner: Segment for enterprise, RudderStack for developers

  • Segment = Fully managed, extensive integrations, enterprise support
  • RudderStack = Open-source option, more technical control, lower cost
  • Both offer similar core functionality

RudderStack is popular among engineering teams that want more control. Segment is better for teams that want to move fast without managing infrastructure.
