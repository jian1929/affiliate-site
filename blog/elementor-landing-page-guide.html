High-Converting Landing Page Structure

Every high-converting landing page follows a proven structure:

  1. Hero Section - Headline, subheadline, primary CTA
  2. Problem/Agitation - Address the pain point
  3. Solution - Present your offer
  4. Features/Benefits - What makes you different
  5. Social Proof - Testimonials, logos, reviews
  6. Objection Handling - FAQs, guarantees
  7. Final CTA - Urgency-driven closing

Pro Tip: Keep It Focused

Landing pages should have ONE goal. Remove navigation, sidebar links, and any distractions that take visitors away from your CTA. Elementor makes this easy with its full-width sections and section-level navigation hiding.

Building the Hero Section

The hero section is the first thing visitors see. It must immediately communicate value.

Essential Hero Elements

  • Compelling Headline - Maximum 10 words, focus on the benefit
  • Supporting Subheadline - Expand on the promise, 1-2 sentences
  • Hero Image/Video - Show your product or result
  • Primary CTA Button - Above the fold, impossible to miss

Hero Section in Elementor

  1. Add a full-width section
  2. Set minimum height to 80vh (viewport height)
  3. Add a Heading widget for your headline
  4. Add a Text Editor widget for the subheadline
  5. Add a Button widget with your CTA
  6. Set background image or video on the section

Adding Social Proof

Social proof builds trust and removes buying objections. Include:

Social Proof Elements to Add

  • Customer testimonials with photos and names
  • Company logos (if you have enterprise clients)
  • Star ratings and review counts
  • Case study previews
  • User-generated content screenshots
  • Media mentions and awards

Elementor Widgets for Social Proof

  • Testimonial Carousel - Display rotating testimonials (Pro)
  • Image Carousel - Client logos and user photos
  • Star Rating - Show review scores prominently
  • Icon List - Display stats (10,000+ users, 4.9 rating)

Creating Effective CTAs

Your call-to-action is where conversions happen. Make it irresistible:

CTA Best Practices

  • Action-oriented text - "Get Started Free" not "Submit"
  • Contrasting color - Stand out from the page
  • Whitespace - Give the button breathing room
  • Benefit-focused - "Get My Free Trial" not "Click Here"
  • Urgency elements - "Limited Time" or "Only 3 Spots Left"

Button Styling in Elementor

  1. Select your Button widget
  2. In Style tab, choose a color that contrasts with your background
  3. Add a subtle shadow for depth
  4. Set hover effects (darker color, slight lift)
  5. Add an icon (arrow) to indicate action

Optimization Tips

Build it, then optimize. A/B test these elements:

  • Headline variations - Benefit-led vs. problem-led
  • CTA color and text - Test at least 3 combinations
  • Hero image vs. video - Videos often convert better
  • Form length - More fields = more qualified leads but fewer submissions
  • Social proof placement - Above vs. below the fold
