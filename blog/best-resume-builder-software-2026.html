Frequently Asked Questions

Are resume builders worth paying for?

Yes, if you're serious about your job search. Free templates from Word or Google Docs often fail ATS screening. Paid builders optimize for both human readers and ATS. The cost (typically $20-50) is a worthwhile investment when landing a job worth tens of thousands annually.

Will recruiters notice I'm using a resume builder?

No—modern resume builders create professional documents indistinguishable from custom-designed resumes. Novorresume and Resume.io produce output that matches professionally designed documents. The key is choosing an appropriate template for your industry.

What format should I download my resume in?

Always provide both PDF and Word formats. PDF preserves formatting across all devices and is best for email attachments. Word allows recruiters to edit if needed. Many ATS systems prefer Word, so having both ready is wise.

Should I use the same resume for all job applications?

No—customize your resume for each application. Highlight experiences and skills most relevant to the specific job description. Most resume builders make it easy to save multiple versions. ATS systems often score resumes based on keyword matches.

What makes a resume ATS-friendly?

ATS-friendly resumes use standard section headings (Experience, Education, Skills), avoid tables and graphics, use common fonts, and include keywords from the job description. All top resume builders optimize for ATS by default.
