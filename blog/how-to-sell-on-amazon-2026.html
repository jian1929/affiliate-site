Why Sell on Amazon in 2026

Amazon remains the world's largest online marketplace, with over 197 million monthly active users and $600+ billion in marketplace sales. For entrepreneurs looking to start an e-commerce business, Amazon offers unparalleled reach without the need to build your own traffic or fulfillment infrastructure.

The Amazon Advantage

  • Built-in Traffic: 66% of US consumers start product searches on Amazon
  • Prime Trust: 200+ million Prime members expect fast, reliable shipping
  • Global Reach: Access customers in 200+ countries through one platform
  • FBA Infrastructure: Amazon handles storage, picking, packing, and shipping

Whether you're looking to generate side income or build a full-time business, selling on Amazon in 2026 offers clear pathways to customers worldwide. But success requires strategy—the days of "throw products up and hope they sell" are long gone.

Recommendation for Beginners

Start with FBA. While it costs more, the Prime badge, customer service, and time savings typically outweigh the fees for new sellers. You can always transition to hybrid models as you scale.

Step 1: Getting Started on Amazon

1 Choose Your Seller Plan

Amazon offers two seller plans:

  • Individual: $0.99 per item sold + referral fees. Best for testing.
  • Professional: $39.99/month + referral fees. Best for serious sellers (unlimited items, API access, bulk tools).

Recommendation: Choose Professional if you plan to sell more than 40 items monthly—the $39.99 fee quickly pays for itself with the time saved on per-item fees.

2 Register Your Account

Visit Amazon Seller Central and complete registration. You'll need:

  • Business email address
  • Government-issued ID
  • Credit card for fees
  • Bank account for payouts
  • Tax information (EIN or SSN)

3 Complete Your Profile

Add your business information, return policy, and customer service email. First impressions matter—even small details like a professional store name build trust.

Step 2: Product Research Fundamentals

Product research is the difference between a profitable business and one that eats your savings. Here's how to find winning products:

Ideal Product Criteria for Beginners

  • ☑ Price between $25-$75 (high enough for profit, low enough for impulse buys)
  • ☑ Weight under 3 lbs (keeps shipping and storage fees low)
  • ☑ Not fragile, hazardous, or easily broken
  • ☑ Has consistent demand (avoid seasonal spikes)
  • ☑ Low competition OR clear differentiation opportunity
  • ☑ Can source for under 30% of selling price

Validate Demand

Before committing, validate that real customers want your product:

  • Search volume: Does the main keyword get 500+ monthly searches?
  • Review counts: Top products with 100-500 reviews show established demand
  • BSR (Best Seller Rank): Lower BSR = higher sales. Under 100,000 in category is decent for beginners
  • Price stability: Are similar products consistently priced $25-75?

Step 3: Sourcing Your Products

Once you've found a product, source it from manufacturers or wholesalers. Here are your main options:

Option 1: Alibaba

The most common sourcing platform for Amazon private label. Find manufacturers, request samples, and negotiate prices.

  • Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ): Usually 500-1000 units for custom products
  • Lead time: 2-4 weeks production + 2-4 weeks shipping
  • Sample costs: $50-200 per sample, usually refundable on bulk orders

Option 2: Wholesale

Buy brand-name products in bulk and resell. Lower risk but thinner margins.

  • Requires: Wholesale account or distributor relationship
  • Margins: Typically 15-30%
  • Advantage: No custom branding, faster to start

Common Sourcing Mistakes to Avoid

  • Ordering samples without testing quality thoroughly
  • Skipping packaging and branding tests
  • Not calculating landed costs before committing
  • Rushing production without QC (quality control) checks

Step 4: Creating Your First Listing

Your listing is your storefront. A great listing converts browsers into buyers. Here's what you need:

1. Compelling Title (200 characters max)

Include: Brand + Product Name + Key Features + Size/Quantity + Primary Keyword

Example: AcmeBrand Kitchen Scale, Digital Food Scale for Baking and Cooking, Stainless Steel, 11lb Capacity, Easy Clean, Auto-Off Feature

2. High-Quality Images

You need 7-10 images. The first (main) image must be pure white background. Include:

  • Main product on white background
  • Lifestyle images showing product in use
  • Infographic with key features
  • Size comparison (ruler, coin, person)
  • Packaging images (if relevant)

3. Backend Keywords

Hidden keywords that help Amazon's search. Don't repeat title words. Include:

  • Synonyms and alternate names
  • Misspellings (Amazon auto-corrects but doesn't always)
  • Targeted long-tail keywords
  • Different languages (if applicable)

4. A+ Content (Enhanced Brand Content)

If you have a registered brand, A+ content dramatically improves conversion. Add comparison charts, lifestyle imagery, and brand storytelling.

Step 5: Launching Your Product

You've sourced, shipped, and listed. Now what? A launch strategy is essential—Amazon's algorithm rewards early momentum.

Launch Checklist

  • ☑ Request initial reviews (Amazon Early Reviewer Program, or use a service like Salesbacker)
  • ☑ Set up PPC campaigns (start with automatic campaigns, $10-20/day)
  • ☑ Drive external traffic (social media, email list, influencer partnerships)
  • ☑ Monitor and optimize (check daily for first 2 weeks)
  • ☑ Gather feedback and iterate

Profit Margin Reality Check

Most successful Amazon sellers target 30-40% net margins. If you're buying a product for $10 and selling for $30:

  • Product cost: $10
  • Shipping to Amazon: $2
  • FBA fulfillment: $4
  • Referral fee (15%): $4.50
  • Total costs: $20.50

Scaling Your Business

Your first product is profitable. Now what? Here are proven scaling strategies:

1. Expand Your Product Line

Once you have one winning product, look for complementary items. Same customer base, cross-sell opportunities, shared storage space.

2. Optimize Your PPC

As you gather data, refine your campaigns. Move from automatic to manual targeting. Focus on high-converting keywords.

3. Build Your Brand

Register your trademark, create A+ content, build a following outside Amazon. Reduces dependency on any one platform.

4. Automate Operations

Use tools for repricing, inventory alerts, and customer service. Your time is better spent on strategy.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much money do I need to start selling on Amazon?

Aim for $2,000-5,000 minimum for your first private label product. This covers product samples, initial inventory (100-200 units), shipping to Amazon, and marketing. You can start smaller with retail arbitrage or wholesale, but private label offers better long-term margins.

Can I sell on Amazon without FBA?

Yes! FBM (Fulfilled by Merchant) lets you ship directly to customers. However, you'll lose Prime eligibility, which hurts your Buy Box chances. FBM works best for oversized items or products with high returns.

How long does it take to make my first sale?

Most new sellers make their first sale within 1-4 weeks of listing. The key is to launch with reviews and drive initial traffic through PPC or external channels.

Is Amazon selling worth it in 2026?

Yes—with the right strategy. Competition has increased, but demand has too. Success requires thorough product research, optimization, and patience. The sellers who fail are usually those who skip the research phase.

Do I need a business license to sell on Amazon?

Not necessarily. Individual sellers can use their Social Security Number. However, if you form an LLC or corporation (recommended for liability protection), you'll use your EIN instead.
