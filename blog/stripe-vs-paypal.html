Pricing Comparison

Stripe Pricing

  • Online Transactions: 2.9% + $0.30 per successful card charge
  • International Cards: Additional 1%
  • Currency Conversion: 1% above the exchange rate
  • ACH Credit: 0.8% (capped at $5)
  • ACH Debit: 0.8% (capped at $5)
  • Wire Transfers: $15 outgoing, $15 incoming
  • Refunds: No fees (only the 2.9% + 30¢ not refunded)

PayPal Pricing

  • Online Transactions: 2.99% + $0.30 per transaction
  • Micropayments: 5% + $0.05 (under $10)
  • International Transactions: Additional 1.5%
  • Currency Conversion: 3-4% above the interbank rate
  • QR Code Payments: 1.9% + $0.10 (in-person)
  • Braintree: 2.59% + $0.30 (for merchants needing more)

💡 Winner by Pricing: Stripe

Stripe is slightly cheaper on standard transactions and significantly better on currency conversion rates. For international businesses, Stripe saves you 2-3% on currency conversions alone.

Features Comparison

Developer Experience

Stripe is widely considered the developer-friendliest payment platform:

  • Beautiful, pre-built UI components (Stripe Elements)
  • Comprehensive documentation
  • SDKs for 13+ programming languages
  • Test mode with instant results
  • Webhook debugging tools
  • Radar fraud prevention built-in
  • Atlas startup program with Stripe

PayPal has improved but trails Stripe:

  • PayPal Checkout (simple buttons)
  • REST APIs available
  • Good documentation but complex integration
  • PayPal Commerce Platform for platforms
  • Braintree for developers needing flexibility

Fraud Prevention

Stripe Radar:

  • Machine learning fraud detection included (no add-on cost)
  • Block by country, card type, risk rules
  • Custom rules engine
  • 3D Secure 2 (SCA compliant)
  • Signature verification for high-risk transactions

PayPal Fraud Protection:

  • Seller Protection included (eligibility criteria)
  • Advanced fraud tools on higher tiers
  • Chargeback protection available
  • 3D Secure 2 support

Pros and Cons

Stripe Advantages

  • Better developer experience
  • Lower currency conversion rates
  • Customizable checkout (no redirect)
  • Built-in fraud prevention
  • Modern dashboard and analytics
  • Billing, Invoicing, Connect built-in
  • Better for SaaS and subscriptions

Stripe Disadvantages

  • Less brand recognition
  • Customer must enter card details
  • Account freezes can happen
  • No phone support (email only)
  • Limited B2B features

PayPal Advantages

  • Highest customer trust
  • 2.2 billion active users
  • Better international recognition
  • Strong seller protection
  • Venmo integration
  • Easy setup (redirect checkout)
  • Phone support available

PayPal Disadvantages

  • Higher fees (especially international)
  • Poorer currency conversion rates
  • Redirect can reduce conversion
  • Account restrictions common
  • Complex pricing tiers
  • Older developer tools

Use Case Recommendations

Choose Stripe if:

  • You're a developer building custom integrations
  • You run a SaaS or subscription business
  • You sell internationally and want lower currency costs
  • You need embedded checkout for better UX
  • You want built-in billing, invoicing, or marketplace features
  • You're building a platform or connecting multiple sellers

Choose PayPal if:

  • You sell to mainstream consumers who trust PayPal
  • You're selling internationally in markets where PayPal is dominant
  • You want strong seller protection
  • You need phone support
  • You're a small business wanting simple setup
  • You want access to Venmo's younger user base

The Hybrid Approach

Many successful e-commerce stores offer both Stripe and PayPal:

  • Stripe for credit card payments (better rates)
  • PayPal for customers who prefer PayPal checkout
  • Studies show 10-20% of customers prefer to pay with PayPal
  • Implementation is straightforward on most platforms

This approach maximizes conversion by giving customers their preferred payment method while optimizing costs for your business.

Conclusion

Both Stripe and PayPal are excellent payment processors in 2026. Stripe wins on pricing, developer experience, and international business. PayPal wins on customer trust, brand recognition, and seller protection.

For most e-commerce businesses, we recommend offering both. The small additional integration effort pays off in increased conversions from customers who prefer one over the other.

If you can only choose one:

  • SaaS, subscriptions, custom integrations → Stripe
  • Mainstream e-commerce, consumer trust → PayPal
