Q4: The Most Important Season

Q4 accounts for 40-50% of most sellers' annual revenue. Getting Q4 right can make your entire year.

Q4 Peak Events

  • Black Friday (Nov 24): Deals on electronics, home goods, toys
  • Cyber Monday (Nov 27): Strongest online deals day of the year
  • Green Monday (Dec 9): Last major shopping day for holiday delivery
  • Free Shipping Day (Dec 14): Final shipping cutoff for many

Q4 Pricing Strategy

October — Prepare and Position:

  • Increase inventory levels (2-3x normal)
  • Start gradual price increases
  • Run modest promotions to build rank
  • Prepare Lightning Deals for Black Friday

November — The Surge:

  • Increase prices 15-30% for BFCM week
  • Competition for Buy Box is fierce
  • Consider matching competitor prices
  • Monitor inventory constantly

December — Hold and Liquidate:

  • Maintain high prices until Dec 15
  • After Dec 15, begin clearance pricing
  • Avoid long-term storage fees at all costs
  • Consider FBA liquidation for slow movers

Q4 Price Increase Math

Let's say your product sells 100 units/month at $29.99:

  • Normal revenue: $2,999/month
  • Q4 with 20% increase ($35.99): $3,599/month
  • Same units, 3 months: +$1,800 extra revenue

A 20% price increase during Q4 can add $1,800-$5,000+ to your annual revenue depending on volume.

Prime Day Strategy

Prime Day (typically mid-July) is Amazon's biggest shopping event. Here's how to capitalize:

Prime Day 2026 Predictions

  • Date: July 15-16 (based on previous years)
  • Expected sales: 2-5x normal daily volume
  • Key categories: Electronics, home, Alexa devices, Prime Exclusive deals

Prime Day Preparation

60 Days Before:

  • Apply for Prime Day Lightning Deals
  • Stock up on inventory
  • Prepare additional PPC budget (3-5x normal)
  • Plan coupon/deal strategy

30 Days Before:

  • Confirm inventory arrives in FBA warehouses
  • Set up automated repricing
  • Create Prime Day-specific PPC campaigns
  • Plan social media promotion

During Prime Day:

  • Monitor prices constantly
  • Adjust PPC bids in real-time
  • Respond quickly to stock issues
  • Capture data for next year

Prime Day Tip: Even if you don't get a Lightning Deal, your products can still benefit from increased traffic. Prepare as if you'll get the deal — because you might get added at the last minute.

Off-Season Strategy (Q1 & Q3)

Off-peak seasons require different tactics:

Q1: Post-Holiday Recovery (January-March)

Q1 Characteristics

  • Lowest traffic of the year (Jan-Feb)
  • Valentine's Day spike (Feb 14)
  • Spring cleaning/prep season begins (March)

Strategy

  • January: Maintain minimal ad spend. Focus on cleanup and planning.
  • February: Valentine's Day promotions for relevant products
  • March: Begin building for spring. Moderate discounts to gain traction.

Q3: The Summer Lull (July-September)

Q3 Characteristics

  • Historically slowest period (July-August)
  • Back-to-school spike (August-September)
  • Prime Day usually kicks off Q4 momentum

Strategy

  • July: Prime Day is your lifeline. Maximize it.
  • August: Back-to-school products surge. Back-to-school isn't just for schools — includes home office, routines, fall prep.
  • September: Begin Q4 prep. Increase inventory and prices gradually.

Dynamic Pricing Strategies

Static pricing is for amateurs. Top sellers use dynamic pricing that responds to:

1. Competitive Pricing

Monitor and match competitors automatically. Tools like Helium 10 and RepricerExpress can automate this.

2. Inventory-Based Pricing

When stock runs low, increase prices. When stock is high, be more competitive.

  • High stock + low velocity: Discount to move inventory
  • Low stock + high velocity: Raise prices to maximize margin
  • Critical stock: Consider removal from sale to protect BSR

3. Time-Based Pricing

Adjust prices based on time of day, day of week, and season.

Competitor Monitoring Strategy

You can't price effectively without knowing what competitors are doing. Set up alerts for:

  • Price changes: Get notified within minutes of competitor price changes
  • Stock status: Know when competitors run out
  • Buy Box wins: Track who wins and why
  • Review changes: Monitor for competitor review velocity

⚠️ Price Alert Setup

Don't wait until Q4 to set up competitor monitoring. Configure alerts now so you're ready when peak season hits. Set alerts for:

  • Price drops below your floor
  • Competitor out of stock
  • Your Buy Box loss
  • Major competitor price changes

Common Seasonal Pricing Mistakes

  1. Pricing too early: Starting discounts in October before BFCM weakens your position
  2. Not raising prices enough: During peak, prices typically go up 15-30% — don't be afraid
  3. Stocking too little: Running out of stock during peak means lost sales AND ranking
  4. Stocking too much: Long-term storage fees in January can destroy Q4 profits
  5. Ignoring competitors: Your pricing strategy must account for what others are doing

The 80/20 Rule of Seasonal Pricing: 80% of your annual profit comes from 20% of the year (Q4 + Prime Day). Plan accordingly and don't waste resources on the other 80% of the year.
