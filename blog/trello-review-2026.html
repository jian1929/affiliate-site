Quick Overview

Trello is a visual project management tool that uses a Kanban-style board system to help teams organize tasks and projects. Acquired by Atlassian in 2017, Trello has maintained its position as one of the most accessible and user-friendly project management tools available, with over 50 million users worldwide.

Bottom Line: Trello remains an excellent choice for individuals, small teams, and organizations seeking a visual, intuitive approach to task management. Its generous free tier and extensibility through Power-Ups make it perfect for those getting started with project management or who need a flexible, collaborative workspace.

Is Trello's Free Tier Enough?

For many teams, Trello's free tier is remarkably capable:

  • Unlimited cards across all boards
  • Up to 10 boards per workspace
  • 250 automation commands per month (using Butler)
  • 250 Power-Up integrations available
  • Unlimited collaborators on any board

Small teams or individuals can often run their entire workflow on the free plan. Upgrade to Standard when you need more boards or larger file attachments.

Key Features

Kanban Boards

Trello's core is the visual Kanban board:

  • Lists: Create columns like "To Do," "In Progress," "Done"
  • Cards: Individual tasks that can be dragged between lists
  • Labels: Color-coded tags for categorization
  • Due Dates: Set deadlines with calendar integration
  • Checklists: Break tasks into subtasks
  • Attachments: Add files, images, and links directly to cards
  • Comments: Collaborate with team members on specific tasks

Multiple Views

With Premium, access additional views beyond the classic board:

  • Timeline: Gantt-style visualization for deadline tracking
  • Dashboard: Card and list activity metrics
  • Map View: Geographic organization (for location-based cards)
  • Calendar: Monthly/weekly view of due dates

Butler Automation

Trello's built-in automation engine (Butler) allows you to:

  • Automate repetitive actions (move cards, assign members, set due dates)
  • Create custom rules based on triggers
  • Schedule recurring tasks
  • Build custom commands and buttons
  • No coding required

Workspace & Organization

Trello's hierarchy organizes your work:

  • Workspaces: Top-level containers for teams or organizations
  • Boards: Individual project or topic boards
  • Cards: Individual tasks or items within lists

Power-Ups: Extending Trello

Power-Ups are integrations that extend Trello's functionality. With 250+ available options, you can transform Trello into a CRM, help desk, or specialized workflow tool.

Popular Power-Ups

  • Calendar: Visual calendar view for due dates
  • Custom Fields: Add text, numbers, dates, and dropdowns to cards
  • Card Repeater: Create recurring tasks automatically
  • Slack: Get notifications and commands in Slack
  • Google Drive: Attach files directly from Drive
  • GitHub: Link commits and PRs to Trello cards
  • Zapier: Connect Trello with 3,000+ apps
Pro Tip: Limit yourself to 3-5 Power-Ups per board. Too many integrations can slow down your board and complicate the workflow. Choose Power-Ups that solve specific problems, not ones that sound interesting.

Final Verdict

Is Trello Right for You?

Trello continues to be one of the most accessible project management tools available. Its visual approach to task management makes it easy for anyone to start organizing their work immediately, while its extensibility through Power-Ups and automation allows it to grow with your needs.

Choose Trello if:

  • You want the easiest project management tool to learn
  • You prefer visual, Kanban-style workflows
  • You need a generous free option
  • You're a freelancer or small team with simple needs
  • You want to customize with Power-Ups
  • Your team values simplicity over features
  • You work with clients who need simple onboarding

Consider alternatives if:

  • You need advanced reporting and analytics
  • Your team uses complex, multi-layered workflows
  • You require detailed time tracking
  • You need enterprise-grade security and compliance
  • You prefer structured methodologies (Scrum, Agile with ceremonies)
  • Your team exceeds 50+ members in a single workspace
