Pricing Breakdown: Asana vs Monday vs ClickUp

Asana Pricing

Asana offers tiered pricing for different team sizes:

  • Basic: Free for up to 15 users
  • Premium: $10.99/user/month - Advanced features, timelines
  • Business: $24.99/user/month - Goals, portfolios, approvals
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing - Advanced security, support

Monday Pricing

Monday.com offers flexible team-based pricing:

  • Free: 2 boards, 3 seats
  • Individual: $9/seat/month - Unlimited boards
  • Basic: $10/seat/month - Integrations, 5GB
  • Standard: $12/seat/month - Timeline, Calendar, 250 automations
  • Pro: $19/seat/month - Analytics, Dashboards, 25,000 automations
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing

ClickUp Pricing

ClickUp offers generous pricing with a forever-free option:

  • Free: Unlimited users, 100MB storage
  • Unlimited: $7/user/month - Unlimited tasks, guests
  • Business: $12/user/month - Custom fields, goals, time tracking
  • Business Plus: $19/user/month - White-labeling, client portals
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing - SSO, advanced security

Features Comparison

Asana Features

Asana excels at complex enterprise workflows:

  • Multiple views: List, Board, Calendar, Timeline, workload
  • Portfolios: Track multiple projects at once
  • Goals: Set and track company and team goals
  • Forms: Custom intake forms for requests
  • Automation: Rules engine for repetitive tasks
  • Proofing: Review and approve creative work
  • Resource management: Track team capacity
  • Time tracking: Built-in time tracking

Monday Features

Monday.com offers a visually appealing and flexible platform:

  • 8+ view types: Board, List, Map, Chart, Timeline, Calendar
  • Workdocs: Collaborative documents
  • Automations: Visual automation builder (250+ actions)
  • Integrations: Native integrations with popular tools
  • Dashboards: Customizable project dashboards
  • Mobile apps: Full-featured iOS and Android apps
  • Guest access: Work with external stakeholders

ClickUp Features

ClickUp is known for being the most feature-complete option:

  • 15+ views: List, Board, Box, Calendar, Gantt, Mind Map
  • Docs: Notion-like documents and wikis
  • Whiteboards: Visual collaboration canvas
  • Goals: OKR tracking and goal hierarchy
  • Time tracking: Built-in with timesheets
  • Screenshots: Native screen recording
  • Custom roles: Granular permission control
  • Relationships: Link tasks across projects

Usability & Interface

Asana Usability

Asana has a clean, professional interface:

  • Learning curve: Moderate - feature-rich but organized
  • Mobile apps: Excellent iOS and Android apps
  • Onboarding: Good tutorials and templates
  • Customization: Good but not overwhelming

Monday Usability

Monday.com prioritizes visual appeal and ease of use:

  • Learning curve: Easy - intuitive drag-and-drop
  • Mobile apps: Full-featured mobile experience
  • Onboarding: Excellent templates and tutorials
  • Customization: Highly customizable boards

ClickUp Usability

ClickUp has the most features, which can be overwhelming:

  • Learning curve: Steeper due to extensive features
  • Mobile apps: Capable mobile apps
  • Onboarding: Good tutorials but complex
  • Customization: Extremely customizable with space defaults

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I migrate between tools?

Yes, all three offer import options. ClickUp has the best migration tools. Expect some manual adjustment regardless of which you switch from.

Which has the best free plan?

ClickUp has the best free plan with unlimited users. Monday.com's free plan is very limited. Asana's free plan supports 15 users.

Which is easiest for non-technical teams?

Monday.com is the easiest to learn. ClickUp has more features but a steeper learning curve. Asana falls in between.

Do they offer offline access?

All three have mobile apps with offline capabilities, though features may be limited offline.
