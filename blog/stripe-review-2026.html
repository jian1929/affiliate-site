What is Stripe?

Founded in 2010 by Irish entrepreneurs Patrick and John Collison, Stripe has become the payment processor of choice for internet businesses. Headquartered in San Francisco with offices worldwide, Stripe processes hundreds of billions of dollars in payments annually for millions of businesses ranging from startups to enterprise corporations.

Unlike traditional payment processors that often feel dated and clunky, Stripe built its platform from the ground up for the modern internet economy. Their elegant API, developer-friendly documentation, and comprehensive toolkit have made Stripe the go-to choice for tech-savvy businesses, marketplaces, and SaaS companies.

Key Takeaway: Stripe is the premium payment processing solution for businesses that prioritize developer experience, want maximum flexibility, and operate primarily online. If you value clean integration, robust features, and a modern platform, Stripe delivers consistently.

  • Include built-in validation for card numbers, expiration, and CVC

  • Support 135+ currencies and multiple payment methods
  • Include Apple Pay, Google Pay, and other digital wallets

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does Stripe take to approve an account?

Most Stripe accounts are activated within a few hours of application. The automated review process typically completes within one business day. Some businesses may require additional verification, which can extend the timeline.

Does Stripe charge monthly fees?

No, Stripe operates on a pay-as-you-go model with no monthly fees, setup costs, or maintenance fees. You only pay per transaction.

How does Stripe compare to PayPal for e-commerce?

Stripe generally offers a better developer experience and more flexible API, while PayPal provides broader consumer recognition and easier customer checkout via PayPal accounts. For tech-savvy businesses wanting maximum control, Stripe is superior. For businesses prioritizing customer trust and quick setup, PayPal has advantages.

Can I use Stripe for in-person payments?

Yes, Stripe Terminal extends Stripe's online payment capabilities to physical locations. You can use Stripe's hardware readers or integrate with third-party POS hardware. However, for primarily in-person businesses, Square often offers better rates.

What happens if a customer disputes a charge?

Stripe's dashboard provides tools to manage chargebacks. When a dispute occurs, you'll receive notification and can submit evidence to contest it. Stripe's Radar fraud tools help prevent disputes, and their Disputes API allows automated responses.

How do payouts work with Stripe?

By default, Stripe payouts funds to your bank account within two business days. You can request next-day or instant payouts for an additional fee. Standard payouts are free.
