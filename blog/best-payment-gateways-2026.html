Frequently Asked Questions

What is a payment gateway?

A payment gateway is a technology that captures and transfers payment data from the customer to the acquiring bank. It serves as the bridge between your website and the payment networks (Visa, Mastercard, etc.), encrypting sensitive data and ensuring secure transactions.

How do payment gateway fees work?

Most payment gateways charge a percentage of the transaction plus a fixed fee (e.g., 2.9% + $0.30). This covers the cost of processing, network fees, and gateway services. Some gateways like Authorize.net also charge monthly fees. Volume discounts are often available for high-volume businesses.

What is the difference between a payment gateway and a payment processor?

While often used interchangeably, a payment gateway captures and encrypts card data at checkout (the "door" to the payment system), while a payment processor routes that data through the card networks. Many providers like Stripe offer both functions in a single platform.

Can I use multiple payment gateways?

Yes, many businesses use multiple gateways for different purposes—for example, Stripe for online sales and Square for in-person events. Some platforms make this easier than others. However, managing multiple gateways adds complexity to reconciliation and reporting.

How long does it take to set up a payment gateway?

Stripe can be up and running in minutes with basic integration. Full custom integration may take days to weeks depending on complexity. Traditional gateways like Authorize.net may require more documentation and verification, taking several business days to approval.

What happens if a payment gateway freezes my account?

Payment gateways may freeze accounts suspected of fraud, high-risk industries, or policy violations. This is a common concern with all processors. Stripe generally provides more transparent communication, but any gateway may freeze accounts without warning. Always read terms of service carefully.

Which payment gateway has the lowest fees?

For online transactions, Square offers the lowest standard rate at 2.6% + $0.10. However, Stripe offers more features and better developer tools. Adyen can offer lower rates for high-volume businesses through custom pricing negotiations.

Is Stripe safe and PCI compliant?

Yes, Stripe is PCI DSS Level 1 certified—the highest level of certification. Card data never touches your servers; Stripe handles all tokenization and encryption. This actually makes your business more secure by reducing compliance scope.
