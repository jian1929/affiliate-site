What is PayPal Business?

PayPal needs no introduction. Founded in 1998 as Confinity and later merged with X.com (Elon Musk's online bank), PayPal has become synonymous with online payments. Today, PayPal serves over 400 million active accounts worldwide and processes hundreds of billions in payment volume annually.

PayPal Business provides a comprehensive suite of payment tools designed for merchants ranging from freelancers and small businesses to large enterprises. Its primary advantage remains consumer trust: customers feel more secure completing purchases through PayPal's protected checkout than entering card details on unfamiliar websites.

Key Takeaway: PayPal Business remains the go-to choice for businesses that prioritize customer trust and ease of use over advanced features. Its brand recognition, buyer protection policies, and simple setup make it ideal for small businesses and new e-commerce ventures.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between PayPal Personal and Business?

PayPal Business accounts offer additional features designed for commerce: invoicing, business dashboards, multi-user access, custom branding, and seller protection for physical goods. Business accounts also support higher transaction volumes and additional payment methods.

How long does PayPal take to transfer funds?

Standard transfers to your bank account take 1-3 business days. Instant transfers to eligible debit cards are available for a small fee (1% of transfer amount, capped at $10). PayPal Balance transfers are instant.

Can I use PayPal for in-person sales?

Yes, PayPal offers several in-person solutions: PayPal Zettle for card processing with hardware readers, QR code payments that customers scan with their phone, and PayPal.me links for collecting payments without cards.

Does PayPal charge monthly fees?

PayPal does not charge monthly fees for standard business accounts. You only pay per-transaction fees. However, optional premium services like advanced reporting may have monthly costs.

How does PayPal protect sellers from fraud?

PayPal's Seller Protection covers eligible transactions against unauthorized payments and items not received. The protection requires meeting specific criteria: shipping to confirmed addresses, responding to disputes promptly, and meeting transaction requirements.

Can I accept payments from international customers?

Yes, PayPal supports over 200 countries and 25 currencies. You can invoice customers internationally, and PayPal handles currency conversion. An additional 1.5% fee applies for currency conversion.
