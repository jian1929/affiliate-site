Pricing Breakdown: Mailchimp vs ConvertKit vs Sendinblue

Understanding the cost structure is crucial for budgeting your email marketing. Each platform has different pricing models.

Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp uses a contact-based pricing model with tiered features:

  • Free: 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month, basic features
  • Essentials: $13/month - 500 contacts, unlimited emails, templates
  • Standard: $20/month - Advanced automation, A/B testing, retargeting
  • Premium: $350/month - Advanced segmentation, multivariate testing
  • Max: Custom pricing - Enterprise features and support

Note: Mailchimp charges per contact, not per email sent. Large lists get expensive quickly.

ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit offers creator-focused pricing with generous free tiers:

  • Free: Unlimited landing pages, 1,000 monthly visitors, basic opt-in forms
  • Creator: $9/month - 300 subscribers, unlimited emails, automation
  • Creator Pro: $29/month - 1,000 subscribers, paid newsletters, advanced automation
  • Powerhouse: $59/month - 3,000 subscribers, all features
  • Scale: $99/month - 10,000 subscribers, all features

Sendinblue Pricing

Sendinblue offers unique daily sending limits rather than monthly:

  • Free: 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts
  • Starter: $25/month - 40,000 emails/month, 1,000 contacts
  • Essential: $58/month - Essential + marketing automation
  • Premium: $83/month - Advanced automation, A/B testing, landing pages
  • Enterprise: Custom - Dedicated IP, SLA, custom volume

Key advantage: Sendinblue allows unlimited contacts even on paid plans.

Features Comparison: Mailchimp vs ConvertKit vs Sendinblue

Mailchimp Features

Mailchimp offers the most comprehensive feature set:

  • Email designer: Drag-drop builder with 100+ templates
  • Creative Assistant: AI-powered design suggestions
  • A/B testing: Subject lines, content, send times
  • Customer journey builder: Visual automation flow
  • Website builder: Basic landing pages included
  • Post purchase emails: E-commerce automation
  • Segmentation: Advanced rules-based segments
  • Analytics: Detailed engagement tracking
  • Integrations: 300+ integrations
  • Audience management: Tags, groups, merge fields

ConvertKit Features

ConvertKit is built specifically for content creators:

  • Visual automation builder: Simple, powerful workflows
  • Broadcasts: One-time emails to segments
  • Sequences: Automated email courses
  • Landing pages: High-converting creator-focused
  • Opt-in forms: Inline, pop-up, and slide-in
  • Tags: Custom subscriber organization
  • Segments: Filter subscribers by behavior
  • Paid newsletters: Built-in subscription payments
  • Commerce: Digital product sales integration
  • Courses: Built-in course platform

Sendinblue Features

Sendinblue combines email with additional channels:

  • Email campaigns: Drag-drop editor with templates
  • SMS marketing: Text message campaigns included
  • WhatsApp campaigns: Growing channel support
  • Chat: Live chat for websites
  • CRM: Built-in customer relationship management
  • Marketing automation: Workflows with multiple triggers
  • Transactional emails: API-driven sending
  • Landing pages: Lead capture pages
  • Facebook ads: Ad management integration
  • Segmentation: Behavior-based audience splitting

Our Verdict: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins?

Best for E-commerce: Mailchimp

If you're running an online store, Mailchimp is our recommendation. The e-commerce integrations, abandoned cart automation, and product recommendation features are unmatched. While pricing has increased, the ROI for e-commerce businesses justifies the cost.

Best for Content Creators: ConvertKit

If you're a blogger, YouTuber, podcaster, or online course creator, ConvertKit is the clear winner. Built specifically for creators, it offers paid newsletters, digital product integration, and a clean interface that doesn't overwhelm. The creator community and focus on newsletter-style emails make it perfect for building an audience.

Best for Budget and Multi-Channel: Sendinblue

If you want the most value for money and plan to use SMS or chat, Sendinblue is excellent. The unlimited contacts on all plans and bundled multi-channel marketing make it ideal for growing businesses on a budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I migrate between platforms easily?

Yes, all three platforms support importing from others. ConvertKit and Sendinblue both offer migration tools. You can export your list as CSV and import to any platform.

Which platform has the best deliverability?

Deliverability depends more on your list quality and authentication than the platform. All three have good deliverability when properly set up with SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records.

Can I use these platforms for transactional emails?

Sendinblue excels at transactional emails with dedicated APIs. Mailchimp and ConvertKit are primarily for marketing emails, though both can handle basic transactional needs.

Which platform is easiest to learn?

ConvertKit has the simplest learning curve for beginners. Mailchimp has the most features but steeper learning curve. Sendinblue falls in between.

Do these platforms support SMS marketing?

Sendinblue includes SMS marketing. ConvertKit and Mailchimp focus solely on email. Mailchimp has sunsetted some SMS features.

Conclusion

Choose your email marketing platform based on your primary use case:

  • E-commerce stores: Choose Mailchimp
  • Bloggers and creators: Choose ConvertKit
  • Budget-conscious with SMS needs: Choose Sendinblue
  • Large B2B lists: Consider Sendinblue or Mailchimp
  • Paid newsletter creators: Choose ConvertKit

Remember: the best email marketing platform is one you'll actually use consistently. Start with the free tiers to test before committing to a paid plan.
