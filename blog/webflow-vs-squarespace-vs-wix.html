Detailed Breakdown

Webflow: The Designer's Choice

Webflow is the most powerful builder for those who want design control. It produces clean, semantic HTML and CSS code that you can export and host anywhere. This makes it unique among website builders.

Best for:

  • Web designers building client sites
  • Those wanting to learn web design
  • Projects requiring custom animations
  • Developers prototyping quickly
  • Anyone wanting code ownership

Not ideal for: Complete beginners or those wanting the absolute easiest setup.

Squarespace: The All-in-One Beauty

Squarespace is the most polished all-in-one solution. Their award-winning templates are some of the most beautiful in the industry, and everything works together seamlessly.

Best for:

  • Creative professionals (photographers, designers, artists)
  • Small businesses wanting polish without effort
  • Portfolio websites
  • E-commerce stores (particularly product-focused)
  • Those who want 24/7 support

Not ideal for: Those wanting complete design freedom or custom functionality.

Wix: The Beginner's Friend

Wix is the easiest platform to get started with. Their 900+ templates and drag-and-drop editor make building a website feel like playing with building blocks.

Best for:

  • Complete beginners
  • Restaurants, salons, local businesses
  • Personal blogs and hobbies
  • Quick landing pages
  • Those wanting the most features out of the box

Not ideal for: Those who may need to switch templates later or want to export their code.

Feature Comparison

E-Commerce Capabilities

All three platforms offer e-commerce, but with different strengths:

  • Webflow: Most flexible. Custom checkout, subscription support, membership sites.
  • Squarespace: Most polished. Beautiful product pages, built-in inventory, excellent for most stores.
  • Wix: Most features. Huge app market, many payment options, but can feel bloated.

Blogging Platform

Squarespace has the best out-of-the-box blogging with beautiful templates. Webflow offers more control with its CMS. Wix has basic blogging but limited features.

SEO Capabilities

Webflow wins on SEO control—you can edit meta tags, schema markup, and have full control over URL structure. Squarespace and Wix offer good basic SEO but less customization.

Design Freedom

Webflow > Squarespace > Wix in terms of design freedom. Once you pick a Wix template, you're locked in. Squarespace allows layout changes but with constraints. Webflow lets you build anything.

When to Choose Each Platform

Choose Webflow if:

  • ✓ You have design experience or want to learn
  • ✓ You need custom animations and interactions
  • ✓ You want to export and own your code
  • ✓ You're building a design agency portfolio
  • ✓ You need CMS-powered dynamic content

Choose Squarespace if:

  • ✓ You want beautiful templates without customization
  • ✓ You're a creative professional with a portfolio
  • ✓ You want the easiest all-in-one solution
  • ✓ You need reliable 24/7 support
  • ✓ You value polish over customization

Choose Wix if:

  • ✓ You're a complete beginner
  • ✓ You want the fastest path to a live site
  • ✓ You need extensive app integrations
  • ✓ You want phone support
  • ✓ You're building a simple local business site

The Bottom Line

There's no single "best" platform—it depends entirely on your needs:

  • Designers and developers → Webflow
  • Creative professionals → Squarespace
  • Beginners and locals → Wix

The good news? All three are solid choices that will help you build a professional website. Your decision should come down to how much design control you need versus how much simplicity you want.

Our recommendation: Start with Squarespace if you want the easiest path to a beautiful site. Choose Webflow if you're willing to invest time learning for maximum control.
