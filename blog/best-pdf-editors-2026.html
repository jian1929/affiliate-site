Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between free and paid PDF editors?

Free editors (like browser-based tools or basic viewers) typically allow viewing, simple annotations, and limited editing. Paid tools add full text/image editing, OCR, form creation, batch processing, advanced security, and e-signatures. For business use, paid is almost always worth it.

Can I edit a PDF without Adobe Acrobat?

Yes! PDFelement, Foxit, and Smallpdf all offer excellent PDF editing capabilities. PDFelement in particular offers nearly all of Acrobat's features at a fraction of the cost. The days of needing Acrobat are over for most users.

What is OCR and why do I need it?

OCR (Optical Character Recognition) converts scanned documents and images into editable, searchable text. All the tools listed offer OCR. Adobe Acrobat and Foxit generally have the most accurate OCR, while PDFelement offers excellent AI-powered OCR at a lower price.

How much does Adobe Acrobat cost vs alternatives?

Adobe Acrobat Pro is $22.99/month. PDFelement is $9.99/month (with annual plans available). Nitro starts at $14.95/month. Foxit starts at $12.99/month. All alternatives offer 30-40% affiliate commissions, with PDFelement offering the highest at 40%.

Which PDF editor is best for Mac?

PDFelement and Smallpdf offer excellent Mac experiences. Adobe Acrobat also has a native Mac app. Nitro has limited Mac support. For occasional use, Smallpdf's browser-based interface works perfectly on any platform.
