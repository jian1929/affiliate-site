2. Business Reports Dashboard

This is where serious sellers spend their time. Three key reports:

Sales Dashboard

Track your top-line metrics over time:

  • Session percentage: How many browsers become buyers
  • Units per session: Average items per order
  • Buy Box percentage: How often you're the featured seller
  • Traffic: Where visitors come from

Detail Page Sales and Traffic

See how each product performs:

  • Session count by ASIN
  • Traffic by parent vs. child ASIN
  • Substitution patterns

Inventory Dashboard

Manage your inventory health:

  • Units sold (daily/weekly/monthly)
  • On-hand inventory by SKU
  • Days of supply
  • FBA sell-through rate

3. Advertising Dashboard

If you're running PPC, this dashboard is essential:

Campaign Manager

  • Spend: How much you're spending daily
  • RoAS: Return on Ad Spend (target 3-5x minimum)
  • Clicks: Volume of traffic you're buying
  • CPC: Cost per click by keyword
  • Conversion rate: Are clicks turning into sales?

Healthy PPC Metrics (Targets)

  • ACOS (Advertising Cost of Sales): Under 30%
  • RoAS: Above 3x (5x+ is excellent)
  • CVR: Above 10% (15%+ is great)
  • CPC: Depends on margins—calculate break-even

4. Inventory Planning Dashboard

Never run out of stock—or overstock. This dashboard helps you manage:

  • Restock alerts: When to reorder
  • Surplus inventory: Slow movers
  • Long-term storage: Items about to hit 365 days
  • Estimated storage charges: Upcoming storage fees

Stock-Out Alert!

Every day of stock-out costs you sales AND ranking. Set alerts for 30-45 days of supply and reorder BEFORE you run out.

5. Financial Dashboard

Know your true profitability:

Payments Summary

  • Estimated total revenue
  • Estimated FBA fees
  • Estimated referral fees
  • Next payout date and amount

Fee Preview

See exactly what Amazon is charging before it's deducted:

  • Referral fees by category
  • FBA fulfillment fees
  • Storage fees
  • Returns processing

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to Track

Don't just track revenue. Track these metrics weekly:

Weekly KPI Check

  • ☑ Revenue: Total sales (daily, weekly, monthly)
  • ☑ Units Sold: Volume movement
  • ☑ ACOS: Advertising efficiency
  • ☑ Refund Rate: Should be under 5%
  • ☑ Inventory Sell-Through: Units sold / units received
  • ☑ Session %: Conversion rate
  • ☑ Buy Box %: How often you're winning
  • ☑ Account Health Score: No warnings

Building Your Own Dashboard

For serious sellers, connecting Seller Central data to custom dashboards unlocks powerful insights:

Google Sheets Integration

Use Amazon's SP-API to pull data into Google Sheets. Benefits:

  • Custom metrics and calculations
  • Automatic daily updates
  • Visualizations and charts
  • Shareable with your team

What to Include in Your Custom Dashboard

  • Daily revenue vs. cost (true profit)
  • ACOS by campaign/ad group
  • Inventory days of supply
  • Refund rate by product
  • Seasonal trends
  • Competitor price tracking

Dashboard Best Practice

Review your dashboard at the same time every day. Morning reviews catch overnight issues. Evening reviews catch shipping delays. Consistency is key.

Common Dashboard Mistakes

Mistake #1: Only Looking at Revenue

Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. Track your true profitability by subtracting product cost, FBA fees, PPC spend, and returns.

Mistake #2: Ignoring Account Health

A suspended account means zero sales. Check your account health metrics daily—before they become problems.

Mistake #3: Not Tracking Inventory Metrics

Stock-outs kill your ranking. Monitor days of supply and restock alerts constantly.

Mistake #4: Ignoring PPC Data

Bad PPC can turn profitable products into money losers. Track ACOS weekly and optimize underperformers.

Creating a Daily Review Routine

15-Minute Morning Review

  • ☑ Check yesterday's sales (does it match expectations?)
  • ☑ Review account health (any warnings?)
  • ☑ Check pending orders (any that need attention?)
  • ☑ Review inventory alerts (any restocks needed?)
  • ☑ Scan for unusual activity (refunds, complaints)

Weekly Deep Dive (1 hour)

  • ☑ Analyze sales trends (growing or declining?)
  • ☑ Review PPC performance (ACOS, CTR, CVR)
  • ☑ Check inventory levels (any stock-outs coming?)
  • ☑ Review refund rates (any product issues?)
  • ☑ Calculate true profit (revenue - all costs)
  • ☑ Plan inventory orders for next week

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is my Amazon seller dashboard?

Log into Amazon Seller Central. The dashboard is your home page after login. For specific reports, use the "Reports" menu.

How often should I check my dashboard?

Quick review daily (5-10 minutes). Deep dive weekly (30-60 minutes). Monthly for strategic planning and trend analysis.

What are the most important metrics?

For most sellers: Revenue, Units Sold, ACOS (if running PPC), Refund Rate, and Account Health metrics. Track these weekly minimum.

How do I improve my session percentage?

Session % (conversion rate) improves with better images, optimized titles/bullet points, competitive pricing, and positive reviews. A/B test your listings to find what works.

What's a healthy Buy Box percentage?

Aim for 80%+ Buy Box ownership. If you're below 50%, check your price competitiveness, seller metrics, and FBA status.
