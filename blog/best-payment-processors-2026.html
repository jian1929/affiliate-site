Feature Comparison

Stripe: Developer-First Approach

Stripe excels in providing the most flexible and powerful payment infrastructure:

  • Stripe Elements: Pre-built, customizable UI components for card collection
  • Stripe Billing: Comprehensive subscription management with trials, proration, and metered billing
  • Stripe Connect: Marketplace and platform payments with automatic split settlements
  • Radar: Machine learning fraud protection with custom rules
  • Atlas: Business incorporation services
  • Extensive API: RESTful API with SDKs for every major language

PayPal: Trust and Convenience

PayPal's primary advantage is consumer recognition and trust:

  • Brand Recognition: 400+ million active accounts worldwide
  • Buyer Protection: Industry-leading purchase protection policies
  • Seller Protection: Coverage for eligible unauthorized and item-not-received claims
  • Multiple Payment Methods: PayPal balance, cards, Buy Now Pay Later
  • PayPal.me: Simple payment links for freelancers and small business
  • Invoicing: Built-in professional invoice creation

Square Online: E-commerce + POS

Square Online uniquely combines website building with point-of-sale:

  • Complete E-commerce Platform: Website builder, product management, checkout
  • POS Integration: Seamless sync with Square POS system
  • Unified Inventory: Manage stock across online and physical stores
  • BOPIS: Buy online, pick up in-store functionality
  • Social Selling: Direct Instagram and Facebook integration
  • Genuine Free Plan: No monthly fee with unlimited products

Developer Experience

For businesses with development resources, the API quality matters significantly:

Stripe: The Developer's Choice

Stripe's API is widely considered the gold standard for payment APIs. With comprehensive documentation, SDKs for every major language, and a test mode that mirrors production exactly, Stripe makes integration as smooth as possible. The Stripe CLI allows local development with webhook forwarding, and their documentation includes runnable code examples.

Best for: Custom integrations, marketplaces, SaaS billing, any developer-heavy environment.

PayPal: Functional but Basic

PayPal's API is functional and supports most use cases, but it lacks the elegance and consistency of Stripe's API. Documentation is comprehensive but can be harder to navigate. The Braintree SDK offers a more developer-friendly experience within the PayPal ecosystem.

Best for: Businesses that need PayPal specifically but have development resources to handle integration complexity.

Square: Good for Simple Needs

Square's API is well-documented and easier than PayPal but less powerful than Stripe. For basic e-commerce needs, it's more than sufficient. The Square App Marketplace offers pre-built integrations for common platforms.

Best for: Businesses using Square POS that need basic online payment integration.

Best For: Use Case Scenarios

Choose Stripe if:

  • You're building a custom e-commerce application
  • You offer subscription or usage-based pricing (SaaS)
  • You're building a marketplace or platform
  • Developer experience and API quality are priorities
  • You need to accept payments from global customers
  • You want the latest features and continuous innovation

Choose PayPal if:

  • Customer trust and brand recognition matter most
  • You want simple setup with minimal technical knowledge
  • You're selling to consumers who prefer PayPal
  • You need strong seller protection policies
  • You want built-in invoicing and payment links
  • You're a freelancer or service-based business

Choose Square Online if:

  • You have physical retail locations alongside online sales
  • You're already using Square POS
  • You want an all-in-one e-commerce platform
  • You need unified inventory across channels
  • You're on a tight budget and want a free option
  • You sell on Instagram or Facebook

Consider Alternatives:

  • Braintree: If you need PayPal features with better developer tools
  • Adyen: If you're an enterprise with complex global needs
  • WooCommerce Payments: If you're deeply invested in WordPress
  • Shopify Payments: If you're on Shopify and want the simplest integration

Final Recommendation

The Bottom Line

There's no single "best" payment processor for everyone. The right choice depends entirely on your business model, technical resources, and priorities.

Stripe is our top recommendation for online businesses, SaaS companies, and developers who value flexibility and features. PayPal remains excellent for businesses prioritizing consumer trust and simple setup. Square Online is the clear winner for omnichannel retailers with physical and online presence.
