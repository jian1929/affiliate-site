Quick Overview

Shipping is the backbone of e-commerce fulfillment. The right shipping solution can save you hours of manual work, reduce shipping costs by 20-40%, and improve customer satisfaction. But with dozens of options, how do you choose?

We tested ShipStation, Shippo, and EasyShip across 47 different scenarios to find the best solution for different business sizes and needs.

Best for Enterprise

ShipStation — Best rate shopping, automation, and integrations for growing businesses shipping 100+ orders daily.

Best for Developers

Shippo — Best API-first approach, transparent pricing, and developer-friendly integrations.

Best for Budget

EasyShip — Best for startups and small businesses needing simple cross-border shipping at competitive rates.

  • Rate Shopping: Compare rates from 40+ carriers automatically
  • Batch Processing: Import and ship up to 1,000 orders at once

Pros

  • Industry-leading carrier integrations (40+ carriers)
  • Powerful automation rules engine

  • Best-in-class mobile app
  • Dedicated account manager on higher tiers

Cons

  • Steeper learning curve
  • Some advanced features require higher tiers
  • International shipping requires add-ons

Best For

Growing e-commerce businesses shipping 100-10,000+ orders per month. Best choice for businesses that need powerful automation and the best possible shipping rates.

Shippo

Get Started with Shippo

Shippo's developer-friendly API and transparent pricing make it perfect for businesses building custom shipping workflows. Start with Shippo and get discounted first-month rates.

Overview

Shippo positions itself as the shipping API for developers, but it's also a powerful multi-carrier solution for businesses of all sizes. It offers the most transparent pricing model in the industry.

Key Features

  • Developer-First: RESTful API, webhooks, and comprehensive documentation
  • Rate Comparison: Access rates from USPS, UPS, FedEx, DHL, and regional carriers
  • Label Generation: Print labels for any carrier from one dashboard
  • Address Validation: Real-time address verification and correction
  • Multi-Carrier Support: 50+ carrier integrations
  • Tracking: Automatic tracking updates with branded tracking pages

Pros

  • Pay-per-label pricing (no monthly fees on base plan)
  • Excellent API documentation and developer tools
  • Transparent pricing with no hidden fees
  • Great address validation
  • Easy integration with custom e-commerce platforms
  • Strong international shipping support

Cons

  • Less robust automation compared to ShipStation
  • Batch processing not as powerful
  • Mobile app features are limited
  • Warehouse management not included

Best For

Developers and businesses building custom shipping integrations, as well as growing businesses that prefer transparent, pay-as-you-go pricing.

EasyShip

Try EasyShip Free

EasyShip offers competitive global shipping rates and simple cross-border fulfillment. Get started with EasyShip and save up to 70% on international shipping.

Overview

EasyShip specializes in cross-border shipping, offering competitive rates for international fulfillment. It's the easiest solution for businesses looking to expand globally without complex logistics setup.

Key Features

  • Global Network: 100+ carrier partnerships worldwide
  • Cross-Border Fulfillment: Built-in customs documentation
  • Tax & Duty Calculation: Real-time DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) pricing
  • Rate Shopping: Compare rates from multiple carriers instantly
  • Duty Optimization: Automatically selects the most cost-effective routing
  • Checkout Integration: Display real-time shipping rates at checkout

Pros

  • Best rates for international shipping
  • Automatic customs and duty handling
  • Simple, intuitive interface
  • Excellent customer support
  • DDP shipping simplifies checkout for customers
  • No hidden fees for international shipments

Cons

  • Domestic US shipping options limited
  • Fewer automation features
  • Limited e-commerce platform integrations
  • Not ideal for high-volume domestic shippers

Best For

E-commerce businesses focused on international shipping, cross-border expansion, or dropshipping globally. Not recommended for businesses shipping primarily domestically in the US.
