What is Amazon Brand Registry

Amazon Brand Registry is a free program that gives brand owners access to powerful tools for protecting and promoting their intellectual property on Amazon. With over 200,000 active brands enrolled, it's become essential for serious sellers.

The program has evolved significantly since its launch in 2015. In 2026, Brand Registry offers two tiers: basic enrollment (free) and Amazon Brand Registry+ (invite-only for high-value brands with advanced protection needs).

Eligibility Requirements

To enroll in Brand Registry, you must have an active registered trademark. Amazon accepts trademarks from:

Accepted Trademark Types

  • Word marks — Text-only trademarks (e.g., "NIKE")
  • Design marks — Logos with text or design elements
  • Standard character marks — Any text, numbers, or letters

Trademark Requirements

Your trademark must be:

  • Text-based or image-based with text elements
  • Live (registered) — Pending applications don't qualify
  • Government-registered — In your country of operation
  • On Amazon's accepted list — Most major trademark offices accepted

💡 Note: Amazon accepts trademarks from USPTO (US), EUIPO (EU), UKIPO (UK), IPO (Canada), CNIPA (China), INPI (France/Brazil), DPMA (Germany), and many others. Check Amazon's full list for your jurisdiction.

Additional Requirements

  • Active Seller Central account in good standing
  • Manufacturer or brand owner (or authorized to sell)
  • Products must have unique identifiers (GTIN/UPC/EAN)
  • Ability to upload product data via XML or spreadsheets

⚠️ Important: Amazon does not accept pending trademark applications. You must have a registered trademark before applying. The registration process typically takes 8-12 months via USPTO or 4-6 months via expedited options.

How to Register Your Brand

The registration process has been streamlined but still requires preparation:

Step 1: Prepare Your Trademark Information

  • Trademark registration number
  • Trademark office (e.g., USPTO, EUIPO)
  • Trademark owner information
  • Product categories you sell
  • Countries where you manufacture and ship

Step 2: Gather Product Data

  • List of ASINs you'll enroll
  • Global Catalog IDs (GCIDs) for older products
  • Product images showing your brand logo
  • Invoice from manufacturer showing brand relationship

Step 3: Apply Through Brand Registry

  1. Go to brandservices.amazon.com
  2. Click "Enroll a Brand"
  3. Select your trademark office and enter registration number
  4. Verify your relationship to the brand
  5. Submit and wait for verification (typically 24-72 hours)

Step 4: Post-Enrollment Setup

  • Set up your Brand Dashboard
  • Create or claim your Amazon Store
  • Enroll in A+ Content
  • Configure brand protection settings

Brand Protection Features

Brand Registry's protection features are its core value proposition:

Transparency Program

Product serialization that lets you:

  • Apply unique codes to each unit (via Amazon or third-party)
  • Verify authenticity at distribution
  • Track products through supply chain
  • Identify counterfeits at the source

Project Zero

For qualifying brands (high brand engagement + low infringement rate), Project Zero provides:

  • Self-service counterfeit removal — Remove fakes without reporting to Amazon
  • Automated protections — ML-powered removal of suspected counterfeits
  • Product serialization — Free at higher volumes

Report Infringement Tool

Streamlined reporting for:

  • Trademark infringement
  • Copyright infringement
  • Design patent infringement
  • Fake reviews
  • Unauthorized sellers

Brand Gating

Prevent unauthorized sellers by requiring approval:

  1. Request gating through Brand Registry
  2. Amazon reviews and approves new sellers
  3. Unauthorized listings are automatically blocked

⚠️ Note: Brand Gating has limitations. Some third-party sellers may qualify for exemption. Combine gating with other protection methods for best results.

Pro Tips for Brand Registry Success

1. Register Early

If you're building a brand, register your trademark before launching on Amazon. It can take 8-12 months to get registered, and early registration means immediate protection when you launch.

2. Use GS1 Barcodes

For products you manufacture, use GS1 UPCs and GTINs registered to your company. This creates an official link between you and your products.

3. Document Everything

Keep records of:

  • Trademark registration documentation
  • Manufacturer relationships and invoices
  • Product development and design records
  • Packaging and label specifications

4. Monitor Your Brand

Set up alerts for:

  • New ASINs in your categories
  • Price changes for your products
  • New sellers entering your brand
  • Review velocity changes

5. Respond Quickly to Infringements

When you find infringing content:

  1. Document with screenshots and dates
  2. File report through Brand Registry immediately
  3. Follow up if not resolved within 24-48 hours
  4. Consider legal action for repeat offenders

6. Leverage Brand Analytics

Use Brand Dashboard insights to:

  • Identify underserved customer needs
  • Find keyword opportunities competitors miss
  • Understand demographic targeting for ads
  • Track brand health over time

💡 Final Tip: Brand Registry is an investment in your business's long-term protection and value. Even if you're small today, building a defensible brand position protects against competitors and counterfeiters who target vulnerable listings.
