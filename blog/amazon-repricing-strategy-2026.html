The Repricing Dilemma

Amazon repricing is a double-edged sword. Price too high, lose the Buy Box. Price too low, lose your margins. The right strategy finds the sweet spot between competitiveness and profitability.

What Is Amazon Repricing?

Repricing is automatically adjusting your product prices based on competitor prices, market conditions, or other factors. On Amazon, this typically means trying to win or maintain the Buy Box—the featured offer that gets the majority of sales.

With millions of sellers competing for the same products, manual repricing is impossible. You need automated repricing software that monitors competitors and adjusts prices in real-time.

Understanding the Buy Box

Before discussing strategies, understand how the Buy Box works. Amazon doesn't simply award the Buy Box to the lowest price. They consider:

  • Price (weighted heavily, but not exclusively)
  • Availability (in stock, can ship quickly)
  • Fulfillment method (FBA sellers often have advantage)
  • Seller rating (history of positive feedback)
  • Performance metrics (defect rate, late shipping rate)
  • Response time (customer service)

Repricing Strategy Types

1. Competitive Match HIGH RISK

Automatically match or beat the lowest competitor price. This is the most aggressive strategy.

Pros

  • Maximum Buy Box share
  • Never beaten on price
  • Fast volume growth

Cons

  • Race to the bottom
  • Destroys margins
  • Competitors can outlast you

Best for: Commoditized products with thin margins where volume is the only lever.

2. Beat-Lowest-By (Buffered) MEDIUM RISK

Always be the lowest price, but by a set minimum amount (e.g., $0.01 to $0.10 above the lowest).

Example: If the lowest competitor is $25.00, you price at $25.01 to $25.10 instead of $25.00.

Pros

  • Guaranteed lowest price
  • Slight margin protection
  • Simple to implement

Cons

  • Still a race to the bottom
  • Small buffer may not cover fees

3. Floor-Protected LOW RISK

Automatically reprice within a defined range (floor to ceiling). Only compete when it's profitable to do so.

Example: If your cost is $10 and you need 30% margin, set floor at $14.30. The repricer only lowers price above $14.30.

Pros

  • Never sell at a loss
  • Protected margins
  • Peace of mind

Cons

  • May lose Buy Box
  • Less competitive

Best for: Most sellers. Protects margins while staying competitive.

4. Time-Based (Strategic) LOW RISK

Adjust prices based on time of day, day of week, or seasonal patterns. Raise prices during high-demand periods, lower during slow times.

Pros

  • Capture maximum value
  • Data-driven pricing
  • Seasonal optimization

Cons

  • Requires data analysis
  • Complex to set up

5. AI-Powered Dynamic MEDIUM RISK

Machine learning algorithms analyze market data, competitor behavior, and historical patterns to optimize pricing in real-time.

Pros

  • Data-driven decisions
  • Adapts to market
  • Optimizes for profit

Cons

  • Expensive tools
  • Black box decisions
  • May have learning curve

How to Calculate Your Repricing Floor

Your repricing floor is the lowest price you'll accept. It should cover all costs and maintain minimum acceptable margin.

REPRICING FLOOR = Product Cost + Shipping to Amazon + Referral Fee % + Minimum Margin

Example:
Product Cost: $10.00
Shipping to Amazon: $2.00
Referral Fee (15% of selling price): Assume $4.50
Minimum Margin Target: 20% ($5.00 on $25 selling price)

Minimum Selling Price: $10 + $2 + $4.50 = $16.50
With 20% margin: $16.50 / 0.80 = $20.63
Repricing Floor: $20.63

Repricing Floor Checklist

  • ☑ Calculate your true product cost (including shipping, packaging)
  • ☑ Add FBA fulfillment cost (check Amazon's current rates)
  • ☑ Include referral fees (8-15% depending on category)
  • ☑ Account for returns and refunds (estimate 5-10%)
  • ☑ Add your minimum acceptable margin
  • ☑ Set a buffer for unexpected costs

Repricing Best Practices

1. Set Intelligent Floors

Never reprice below your cost. Calculate your true floor by accounting for ALL costs—product, shipping, fees, returns, and your target margin.

2. Filter Competitors

Not all competitors are equal. Filter out:

  • Sellers with poor ratings
  • Sellers with slow shipping
  • New sellers without history
  • Condition: Used vs. New

3. Adjust for Buy Box Eligibility

If your metrics are strong (low defect rate, fast shipping, high ratings), you don't always need to be the absolute lowest price. Amazon values reliability.

4. Monitor and Optimize

Review your repricing performance weekly. Are you winning the Buy Box? What's your margin? Adjust floors and strategies based on data.

5. Consider Time-of-Day Strategies

Repricing constantly is expensive and unnecessary. Focus repricing intensity during peak buying hours (typically 9am-5pm in your target timezone).

Common Repricing Mistakes

  • No floor set — Selling at a loss to win Buy Box
  • Too aggressive — Competing with clearly unprofitable sellers
  • Not filtering — Competing against poor-rated sellers unnecessarily
  • Ignoring BSR — Competing when you're already dominating the category

When NOT to Reprice

Repricing isn't always the answer. Consider pausing when:

  • You're dominating the category — High BSR means you have pricing power
  • Competitor is selling at a loss — They can't sustain it; wait them out
  • Low stock — Save inventory for full-price sales
  • Seasonal peak — Prime Day, Black Friday, Christmas — maintain higher prices
  • New product launch — Build reviews at full price before competing

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need repricing software?

Yes, if you sell more than 10-20 SKUs or products where competitors frequently reprice. Manual repricing is impossible at scale and you'll miss opportunities.

Will aggressive repricing destroy my margins?

Only if you don't set intelligent floors. Floor-protected repricing ensures you never sell below your cost. The key is calculating accurate floors that account for all fees.

Should I always be the lowest price?

No. Being the lowest price isn't always necessary to win the Buy Box, and it destroys your margins. With strong seller metrics (high ratings, low defect rate, fast shipping), you can price slightly higher and still win the Buy Box.

How often should I reprice?

Modern repricing tools reprice in real-time (minutes). More aggressive strategies may reprice every 5-15 minutes. Less aggressive strategies may only reprice hourly.

What's the best repricing strategy for beginners?

Start with floor-protected competitive matching. This ensures you never sell at a loss while staying competitive. As you learn your market, you can optimize from there.
