What is Amazon FBA?

FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) is a service where you send your products to Amazon's warehouses, and they handle storage, picking, packing, shipping, and even customer service. You focus on sourcing products; Amazon handles the rest.

How it works:

  1. Source products from manufacturers or wholesalers
  2. Ship products to Amazon's fulfillment centers
  3. Amazon stores your inventory
  4. When a customer orders, Amazon picks, packs, and ships
  5. Amazon handles returns and customer service
  6. You receive direct deposits for your sales

Phase 1: Getting Started

Week 1: Account Setup

  1. Create a seller account at sellercentral.amazon.com
  2. Choose Individual ($0.99/item sold) or Professional ($39.99/month)
  3. Complete identity verification
  4. Add a bank account for deposits
  5. Set up two-factor authentication

✅ Account Setup Checklist

  • ☐ Professional seller account (recommended for scaling)
  • ☐ Credit card on file
  • ☐ Bank account linked for direct deposits
  • ☐ Tax information completed
  • ☐ Store policies configured (returns, shipping)

Phase 2: Product Research

This is the most critical phase. The right product can make you money; the wrong product can lose thousands. Here's how to find winners:

What to Look For in a Product

  • Demand: At least 300 monthly sales for top competitors
  • Competition: Not dominated by established brands
  • Weight: Under 2 lbs to minimize shipping costs
  • Size: Small enough to fit standard packaging
  • Price: $15-50 sweet spot for beginners

Phase 3: Sourcing & Shipping

Where to Source Products

  • Alibaba: Direct from manufacturers, lowest prices, longest lead times
  • Global Sources: Alternative to Alibaba with verified suppliers
  • ThomasNet: US-based manufacturers
  • Indie Sources: Makers, artisans, small-batch manufacturers
  • Retail Arbitrage: Buying from retail stores to resell

Shipping to Amazon

  1. Create shipping plan in Seller Central
  2. Choose shipping method: Sea freight (cheapest, slowest), Air (fastest, priciest)
  3. Prepare inventory: Label each unit with FNSKU barcodes
  4. Ship to Amazon: Use Amazon's partnered carriers for best rates

⚠️ Common Sourcing Mistakes

  • Ordering samples before testing quality
  • Not calculating all fees before purchasing
  • Underestimating shipping time
  • Not using a freight forwarder for customs

Phase 4: Listing Optimization

Your listing determines whether browsers become buyers. Here's how to optimize:

Title Formula

Brand + Model + Key Feature + Size/Color + Package Quantity

Example: "ACME Kitchen Timer, Digital Backlight Display, Count Up/Down, Magnetic Back, AAA Battery Included, Silver"

Backend Keywords

Hidden keywords that help with search. Include:

  • Alternative product names
  • Common misspellings
  • Keywords in other languages (if relevant)
  • Targeted search terms

Images Requirements

  • Minimum 6 images (7 preferred)
  • First image: pure white background
  • Show product from multiple angles
  • Include lifestyle images
  • Show size with comparisons
  • Highlight key features

Phase 5: Scaling Your Business

Growth Strategies

  • Expand product line: New products in your category
  • Private label: Create your own brand
  • Raise prices: Increase margins on existing products
  • Reduce PPC spend: Optimize advertising efficiency
  • Add variations: Offer color/size options

Key Metrics to Track

  • Units sold: Daily, weekly, monthly
  • Revenue: Gross and net after fees
  • ACOS: Advertising cost of sales (target under 30%)
  • Best Seller Rank: Category performance
  • Review velocity: New reviews per week

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does FBA cost?

FBA fees vary by product size and weight but typically range from $3-10 per unit. Calculate your specific fees using Amazon's FBA revenue calculator before purchasing inventory.

Is FBA still profitable in 2026?

Yes, but competition has increased. Success requires solid product research, good sourcing, and listing optimization. Commoditized products are harder; differentiated products still thrive.

How long does it take to ship to Amazon?

From China: 2-4 weeks sea freight, 7-10 days air. Plan 4-6 weeks total for first shipment to account for production and transit.

What's the minimum investment to start FBA?

Plan for $3,000-5,000 minimum for first inventory, plus $500-1,000 for tools, labels, and shipping. Starting with less often leads to failure.

Can you do FBA without investing in inventory?

Retail arbitrage (buying from retail stores) requires less upfront capital but is harder to scale. It's a valid starting point but not a long-term strategy.
