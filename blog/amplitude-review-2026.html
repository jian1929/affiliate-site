What Is Amplitude?

Amplitude is the leading product analytics and customer data platform, trusted by over 40,000 companies worldwide including Samsung, Toyota, and Figma. Founded in 2012, Amplitude has become the gold standard for understanding user behavior and driving product-led growth.

Unlike basic web analytics tools, Amplitude provides deep behavioral analytics that help product teams understand how users actually interact with their products. It goes beyond page views to track individual user journeys, identify conversion funnels, and surface insights that drive product decisions.

Why Product Teams Choose Amplitude

Amplitude combines the power of product analytics with a customer data platform (CDP), enabling companies to unify their data, analyze user behavior, and activate insights across marketing and product channels. Its intuitive interface and powerful visualization tools make complex data accessible to the entire organization, not just data scientists.

Amplitude's CDP capabilities allow you to:

Value Proposition

Amplitude's free tier is generous for small teams, but the real value emerges with Growth and Enterprise plans. The platform's ability to unify product analytics with a CDP eliminates the need for separate tools, potentially saving thousands annually on multiple subscriptions.

Amplitude vs The Competition

Amplitude vs Mixpanel

Winner: Amplitude for most teams

  • Amplitude = Broader feature set, CDP capabilities, better collaboration tools
  • Mixpanel = Simpler pricing for event-based models, stronger in some enterprise accounts

Amplitude has pulled ahead with its expansion into the CDP space and superior visualization tools. Mixpanel remains strong but has become more of a niche player.

Amplitude vs Segment

Winner: Amplitude for product teams, Segment for data teams

  • Amplitude = Product analytics + CDP, action-oriented insights
  • Segment = Data infrastructure, warehouse-first approach, more technical

If you're primarily focused on product analytics and activation, Amplitude wins. If you need deep data warehouse integration, Segment (now Twilio Segment) may be better suited.

Amplitude vs Google Analytics 4

Winner: Amplitude for product analytics

  • Amplitude = Deep behavioral analytics, cohort analysis, product-specific metrics
  • GA4 = Website/app traffic, acquisition reporting, free tool

GA4 is great for marketing and acquisition reporting. Amplitude excels at understanding what users do AFTER they arrive. Many companies use both: GA4 for traffic and Amplitude for product analytics.
