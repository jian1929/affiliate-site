Launching a new product on Amazon FBA is high-stakes. Get it right and you'll build momentum that compounds for months. Get it wrong and you'll spend the next 6 months fighting uphill against poor ranking and negative reviews.

This checklist covers everything from pre-launch research to post-launch optimization. Follow it step by step and you'll launch with confidence.

The Four Phases of a Successful Launch

I've organized this checklist into four phases:

  • Phase 1: Pre-Launch Research (Days -60 to -30)
  • Phase 2: Product & Listing Prep (Days -30 to -7)
  • Phase 3: Launch Week (Days -7 to +7)
  • Phase 4: Post-Launch Optimization (Days +7 to +30)

Important: Don't skip Phase 1. Most launch failures happen because sellers rush into products without proper validation. Spend the time upfront to save heartache later.

Phase 1: Pre-Launch Research Days -60 to -30

Validate product demand

Check monthly sales estimates. Target products with 300+ monthly sales in your category.

Analyze competition

Identify the top 10 sellers. Check their reviews, pricing, and BSR. Are they consistently running out of stock?

Calculate unit economics

Product cost + shipping + Amazon fees + PPC + landed cost. Can you still profit at the going rate?

Check patent and trademark

Search USPTO for trademarks. Check Alibaba for generic vs proprietary designs. IP claims kill businesses.

Source samples

Order 2-3 samples from your supplier. Inspect quality, packaging, and shipping time personally.

Identify keyword targets

Use Helium 10 or Jungle Scout to find 20-30 relevant keywords with good search volume and reasonable competition.

Calculate required capital

Inventory + PPC budget + tools + misc = total launch budget. Don't launch undercapitalized.

Phase 2: Product & Listing Prep Days -30 to -7

Order production batch

Order 3-6 months of inventory based on demand projections. Factor in lead time + buffer.

Create product images

Invest in professional photos. Include: main image (white bg), lifestyle shots, infographic, packaging.

Write compelling copy

Title: Primary keyword + brand + key feature + size/color. Backend: All relevant keywords.

Create EBC/A+ content

Enhanced brand content increases conversion 3-10%. Use lifestyle images, comparison charts, brand story.

Set up PPC campaigns

Create auto campaigns + exact/phrase/broad match campaigns. Set budgets but don't activate until launch day.

Build review pipeline

Set up early reviewer program, vine enrollment, or use a compliant review service like Helium 10 Review Automation.

Prepare social proof assets

Prepare unboxing videos, lifestyle content, and influencer partnerships for post-launch.

Set up competitor monitoring

Configure price alerts and competitor tracking so you can respond to changes instantly.

Pre-Launch Traffic Strategy

Don't wait for Amazon to find your product. Build external traffic before launch:

  • Email list? Send a preview to existing customers
  • Social following? Tease the launch on Instagram/TikTok
  • Reddit communities? Share genuinely helpful content (no spam!)
  • Influencers? Consider gifting for honest reviews

Phase 3: Launch Week Days -7 to +7

Confirm inventory arrival

Verify your FBA shipment is received and in "Sellable" status. Test buyability.

Activate PPC campaigns

Turn on campaigns with aggressive bids (110-120% of target ACoS) to gather initial data.

Run lightning deals

If eligible, schedule a Lightning Deal for maximum visibility. Coordinate with PPC.

Monitor pricing

Be ready to reprice competitively. Your goal is initial sales velocity, not margin.

Track BSR hourly

Your Best Sellers Rank shows how you're performing. Lower (better) is the goal.

Respond to questions

Check for buyer questions daily. Fast responses improve conversion.

Drive external traffic

Execute your social/email/influencer plan from Phase 2. Every external sale helps ranking.

The Launch Sweet Spot: Amazon's algorithm gives new products a 2-4 week "newness" boost. Maximize sales during this window — it's the easiest time to rank.

Phase 4: Post-Launch Optimization Days +7 to +30

Analyze PPC data

Download search term reports. Identify winners and losers. Move converting keywords to exact match.

Adjust bids based on ACoS

Lower bids on underperformers. Increase bids on products with good conversion but high ACoS.

Add negative keywords

Block irrelevant searches consuming budget. Check auto campaign reports.

Respond to first reviews

Thank positive reviewers. Address negative reviews professionally. Request corrections if inaccurate.

Test different images

Run an EBC image test. Amazon's A/B testing can reveal which images convert better.

Optimize pricing

Once ranking stabilizes, test price increases. Small increases can significantly boost margin.

Plan next product

Document lessons learned. Start thinking about product #2 while this one is ramping.

Common Launch Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Launching with bad product photos: You get one chance at a first impression. Invest in professional images.
  2. Underpricing too aggressively: Race to the bottom pricing hurts everyone. Match the market.
  3. Ignoring PPC data: Launch week generates gold data. Use it.
  4. Running out of stock: Have 2+ months of inventory ready. Stockouts destroy ranking.
  5. Not requesting reviews: Amazon doesn't automatically email buyers. Use Vine, Early Reviewer Program, or compliant automation.

The #1 Launch Mistake

Not having enough capital. Sellers who run out of money mid-launch can't sustain the PPC spend needed to rank. Calculate your true burn rate and have 6 months of runway.
