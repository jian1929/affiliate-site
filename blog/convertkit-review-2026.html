What is ConvertKit?

ConvertKit is an email marketing platform designed specifically for content creators, including bloggers, YouTubers, podcasters, authors, and online course creators. Founded in 2013 by Nathan Barry, ConvertKit has grown to serve over 700,000 creators, positioning itself as the "creator marketing platform" that understands the unique needs of people who build audiences through content.

Unlike general-purpose email tools or e-commerce focused platforms, ConvertKit is engineered around the creator economy: people who monetize through digital products, courses, affiliate marketing, sponsorships, and audience building rather than direct product sales.

Key Takeaway: ConvertKit is the platform of choice for creators who want to build direct relationships with their audience, sell digital products, and grow sustainable online businesses. If you're a blogger, YouTuber, podcaster, or course creator, ConvertKit's creator-focused features make it worth serious consideration.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does ConvertKit compare to Mailchimp?

ConvertKit is specifically designed for creators, while Mailchimp is more general-purpose. ConvertKit offers unlimited sends, unlimited landing pages, and a simpler interface focused on creator needs. Mailchimp has more design templates and features but can feel bloated. For bloggers, YouTubers, and course creators, ConvertKit is typically the better choice.

Is ConvertKit good for e-commerce?

No, ConvertKit is not designed for e-commerce. If you run an online store with products, carts, and orders, use Klaviyo instead. ConvertKit's commerce features are limited to digital products and subscriptions, not physical goods.

Can I sell courses on ConvertKit?

Yes, ConvertKit (Kit Commerce) lets you sell digital products including courses. However, for complex course platforms with video hosting, quizzes, and certificates, dedicated platforms like Teachable, Thinkific, or Podia may be better. ConvertKit works well for simple digital product delivery.

What's the difference between ConvertKit and Substack?

Substack is specifically for paid newsletters, while ConvertKit is a full email marketing platform. Substack takes 10% of your revenue; ConvertKit has fixed pricing. ConvertKit offers more flexibility with automation, landing pages, and integrations. Substack is simpler but less powerful.

Does ConvertKit have SMS marketing?

No, ConvertKit does not offer SMS marketing. If SMS is important for your business, consider platforms like Klaviyo or Drip that include SMS capabilities. Many creators find email sufficient and appreciate ConvertKit's focus on its core strength.

Can I migrate from another platform to ConvertKit?

Yes, ConvertKit provides migration tools and assistance. You can import subscribers from Mailchimp, AWeber, and other platforms. ConvertKit also offers free migration assistance for creators switching from competitors.
