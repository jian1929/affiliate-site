Pricing Breakdown: Wix vs Squarespace vs WordPress

Understanding the true cost of each platform is essential for budgeting your website. Let's break down the pricing structures in detail.

Wix Pricing

Wix offers tiered pricing with a free option and premium plans:

  • Free: Limited storage, Wix subdomain, ads displayed
  • Combo: $17/month - Connect domain, no ads, 2GB storage
  • Unlimited: $25/month - Unlimited bandwidth, 10GB storage
  • Pro: $32/month - Professional tools, 50GB storage
  • VIP: $59/month - Priority support, 100GB storage
  • Business Basic: $32/month - E-commerce start, payment processing
  • Business Unlimited: $56/month - Full e-commerce, automation
  • Business VIP: $90/month - Advanced e-commerce, priority support

Squarespace Pricing

Squarespace provides clean, all-inclusive pricing tiers:

  • Personal: $16/month - Basic website, no commerce
  • Business: $23/month - Business tools, basic commerce
  • Basic Commerce: $28/month - Full e-commerce, 0% transaction fees
  • Advanced Commerce: $49/month - Advanced commerce, automation
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing - For large organizations

WordPress Pricing

WordPress itself is free, but you'll need hosting and other costs:

  • WordPress.org: Free (open source)
  • Hosting: $3-$50/month (shared to managed WP)
  • Domain: $10-$20/year
  • Premium Themes: $0-$200 (one-time)
  • Premium Plugins: $0-$500/year
  • Maintenance: $0-$100/month (DIY to managed)

Our Verdict: Which Website Builder Wins?

Best for Complete Beginners: Wix

If you're building your first website and want the easiest experience possible, Wix is our top recommendation. The drag-and-drop editor, ADI assistant, and complete lack of technical requirements make it perfect for non-technical users. You can have a professional site up in hours, not days.

Best for Design Quality: Squarespace

If design excellence is your priority and you're willing to work within some constraints, Squarespace is the winner. The templates are consistently beautiful, the platform is stable, and everything works together seamlessly. It's perfect for photographers, artists, and design-focused businesses.

Best for Power and Scale: WordPress

If you need ultimate flexibility, plan to scale large, or want the best SEO potential, WordPress is the clear choice. While it requires more technical knowledge, the power and possibilities are unmatched. Most major websites and businesses ultimately choose WordPress for long-term growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I switch from Wix or Squarespace to WordPress later?

Yes, but it requires manual migration. Export your content, set up WordPress, and import. Design won't transfer, so plan for rebuilding the visual aspects.

Which platform has the best SEO?

WordPress offers the most SEO power with plugins like Yoast. Squarespace provides excellent basics with clean code. Wix has improved significantly but still lags behind.

Can I use my own domain name?

Yes on all platforms. Wix and Squarespace include domain connection; WordPress requires purchasing a domain from a registrar.

Which platform is cheapest for e-commerce?

WordPress with WooCommerce is free. However, consider hosting costs ($5-$50/month). Squarespace's Commerce plans (starting at $28/month) include everything.

Do these platforms own my content?

You own your content on all three. However, you only truly "own" your site with WordPress since you're not dependent on a platform.

Your choice between Wix, Squarespace, and WordPress depends on your priorities:

  • Large businesses with budgets: Choose WordPress

Remember: the best website builder is the one you'll actually use consistently. Choose based on your current skill level and where you want to grow.
