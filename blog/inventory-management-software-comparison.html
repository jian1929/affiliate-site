Why Inventory Management Matters for E-commerce

Effective inventory management is the backbone of any successful e-commerce operation. The difference between a profitable business and one drowning in carrying costs often comes down to how well you track, forecast, and manage your stock.

Key Statistic

Businesses lose 3-5% of their annual revenue to poor inventory management. Stockouts alone account for 65% of lost sales in e-commerce.

The Cost of Getting It Wrong

  • Overstocking: Ties up capital, increases storage costs, risks obsolescence
  • Stockouts: Lost sales, damaged customer relationships, lost rankings
  • Manual Errors: Incorrect counts lead to overselling, shipping delays
  • Lack of Visibility: Not knowing what's where causes fulfillment chaos

What Modern Inventory Software Solves

Today's inventory management platforms address these challenges through:

  • Real-time sync across all sales channels (Amazon, Shopify, eBay, WooCommerce)
  • Demand forecasting powered by historical sales data
  • Automatic reorder points and purchase order generation
  • Multi-location tracking and transfer management
  • Barcode scanning for accurate cycle counts
  • Integration with accounting and fulfillment systems

Cin7 (Formerly Ordable)

4.5/5 Best for: Enterprise e-commerce operations

Cin7 is an all-in-one inventory and order management platform designed for growing e-commerce brands. It connects your sales channels, inventory, and fulfillment operations into a single dashboard.

Key Features

  • Native integrations with 500+ e-commerce platforms and marketplaces
  • B2B and B2C order management
  • Warehouse management with bin locations
  • Manufacturing and assembly tracking
  • POS integration for omnichannel retail
  • Demand planning and forecasting

Pros

  • Comprehensive channel integration (Shopify, Amazon, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, eBay)
  • Flexible enough for both retail and wholesale
  • Strong reporting and analytics
  • Dedicated implementation support

Cons

  • Higher price point than simpler solutions
  • Steeper learning curve
  • Setup requires planning and configuration

Pricing

Starts at $349/month for the Starter plan. Enterprise pricing available for larger operations. View full pricing

TradeGecko (QuickBooks Commerce)

4.3/5 Best for: Growing brands with B2B/B2C mix

QuickBooks Commerce (formerly TradeGecko) combines inventory management with B2B e-commerce capabilities. It's particularly strong for businesses selling both wholesale and direct-to-consumer.

Key Features

  • Multi-channel inventory sync (Shopify, WooCommerce, Amazon, BigCommerce)
  • B2B e-commerce storefront builder
  • Automated purchase orders and reorder points
  • Integrates natively with QuickBooks Online
  • Mobile app for on-the-go inventory checks
  • Workflow automation rules

Pros

  • Excellent QuickBooks integration (accounting seamless)
  • Built-in B2B e-commerce capabilities
  • Clean, intuitive interface
  • Strong automation features

Cons

  • Limited manufacturing features
  • Warehouse management less sophisticated than competitors
  • B2B pricing can add up quickly

Pricing

Plans start at $59/month for Basic. The Professional plan ($199/month) unlocks full features. View pricing details

Fishbowl

4.2/5 Best for: Mid-market brands with warehouse complexity

Fishbowl is a robust inventory management solution with strong manufacturing and warehousing capabilities. It offers both cloud and on-premise deployment options.

Key Features

  • Manufacturing, assembly, and kitting capabilities
  • Lot and serial number tracking
  • Advanced warehouse management with pick-pack-ship
  • Barcode scanning and mobile warehouse support
  • QuickBooks integration (both Online and Desktop)
  • Flexible reporting and custom fields

Pros

  • Powerful manufacturing features not found elsewhere
  • On-premise option for businesses needing full control
  • Excellent QuickBooks sync
  • Highly customizable

Cons

  • Interface feels dated compared to newer SaaS solutions
  • Requires Windows (no native Mac or mobile app)
  • Complex setup for full functionality
  • Cloud version has fewer features than on-premise

Pricing

Fishbowl Go (cloud) starts at $3,150/year. Fishbowl Host (on-premise) requires a quote. Request a quote

Zoho Inventory

4.4/5 Best for: Budget-conscious small businesses

Zoho Inventory offers solid inventory management within the broader Zoho ecosystem. It's an excellent choice for small businesses that want room to grow without significant upfront investment.

Key Features

  • Multi-channel selling (Shopify, Amazon, eBay, WooCommerce, BigCommerce)
  • Automated workflows and rules
  • Batch and serial number tracking
  • Integrated shipping with major carriers
  • Part of Zoho suite (CRM, Books, Commerce)
  • Free plan available for basic needs

Pros

  • Generous free tier for very small businesses
  • Seamless integration with other Zoho apps
  • Affordable pricing with predictable costs
  • Good for businesses transitioning from spreadsheets

Cons

  • Limited advanced features compared to enterprise solutions
  • Warehouse management capabilities are basic
  • B2B e-commerce not as robust
  • May outgrow it as you scale

Pricing

Free plan available. Paid plans start at $29/month for the Basic tier. See all plans

ShipBob

4.5/5 Best for: E-commerce brands needing fulfillment included

ShipBob combines inventory management with third-party logistics (3PL) fulfillment. You ship your stock to their warehouses, and they handle storage, picking, packing, and shipping.

Key Features

  • Integrated inventory management dashboard
  • Multiple fulfillment center locations (US, Canada, UK, EU, Australia)
  • 2-day shipping with Amazon Buy Box eligibility
  • Same-day fulfillment cutoff times
  • Value-added services (kitting, customization, returns)
  • API and Shopify/WooCommerce/Amazon integrations

Pros

  • No warehouse overhead or staff needed
  • Fast, reliable fulfillment included
  • Scalable from startup to enterprise
  • Excellent e-commerce platform integrations

Cons

  • No control over fulfillment operations
  • Storage and fulfillment fees add up
  • Not ideal for fragile or oversized items
  • Minimum order volume requirements

Pricing

Storage fees from $10/month per pallet. Fulfillment from $3.50 per order. Get a custom quote

Inventory Source

4.0/5 Best for: Dropshippers and multi-vendor sellers

Inventory Source specializes in dropship inventory management and automated product syncing. They partner with major distributors to provide real-time inventory feeds.

Key Features

  • Automated product data feeds from 250+ distributors
  • Real-time inventory and pricing updates
  • Integration with Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, 3dcart
  • Automated order routing to suppliers
  • Custom dropship storefront builder
  • UPCEAN barcode database access

Pros

  • Massive distributor network ready to use
  • Fully automated order fulfillment
  • No inventory risk or upfront stock costs
  • Good for testing new products

Cons

  • Lower margins due to dropshipping model
  • Less control over shipping times
  • Quality control challenges with multiple suppliers
  • Competition from other dropshippers

Pricing

Plans from $99/month for Basic, $299/month for Pro. Compare plans
