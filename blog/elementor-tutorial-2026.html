Prerequisites

Before starting this Elementor tutorial, you'll need:

  • A WordPress website (self-hosted, not WordPress.com)
  • Elementor plugin installed and activated (free version works)
  • About 30 minutes of uninterrupted time

Free vs Pro

This tutorial uses the free version of Elementor. The Pro version adds 50+ additional widgets, theme builder, popups, and WooCommerce integration. Learn more in our Elementor Review.

Understanding the Elementor Interface

When you click "Edit with Elementor" on any page, you'll see three main areas:

1. Left Panel (Widgets)

Contains all draggable widgets organized by category: Basic, General, and WordPress widgets.

2. Canvas (Main Area)

Where you build your page. Drag widgets here and arrange them as needed.

3. Bottom Panel (Settings)

Appears when you click an element. Contains Content, Style, and Advanced tabs.

Creating Your First Page

1 Create a New Page

In WordPress dashboard, go to Pages > Add New. Give it a title like "My First Elementor Page."

2 Launch Elementor

Click the blue "Edit with Elementor" button. Wait for the editor to fully load.

3 Add a Section

Click the purple "+" button in the center of the canvas. Choose the number of columns (1 for simple pages, 2 for sidebars).

Pro Tip: Start with Templates

Instead of building from scratch, click the folder icon and browse 100+ free templates. Select one and customize it to your needs. This saves hours of work.

Working with Widgets

Widgets are the building blocks of your Elementor pages. Here's how to use them:

Adding Widgets

  1. Find the widget in the left panel (e.g., Heading, Image, Button)
  2. Drag it to your desired section, or
  3. Click the widget to add it to your section automatically

Essential Widgets for Beginners

Heading

Add titles and subtitles. Change size, color, and typography.

Image

Display photos. Use the styling options to add borders, shadows, and effects.

Text Editor

Write paragraphs with rich formatting. Supports headings, lists, and links.

Button

Create call-to-action buttons. Style with colors, icons, and hover effects.

Styling Your Elements

Every element has three styling tabs:

  • Content - Text, images, and settings specific to the widget
  • Style - Colors, typography, borders, shadows
  • Advanced - Spacing, responsive settings, CSS custom properties

Responsive Design

Use the device icons at the bottom of the canvas (desktop, tablet, mobile) to preview how your page looks on different screen sizes. Make adjustments for each device.

Using Templates

Templates are pre-designed pages or sections you can use as starting points:

  1. Click the folder icon (Browse Templates)
  2. Choose "Page" for full pages, or "Section" for partial layouts
  3. Click a template to preview, then "Insert" to add it

Save Your Own Templates

After building a section you like, right-click it and select "Save as Template." This creates a reusable template for future projects.

Publishing Your Page

1 Save Your Work

Click the arrow next to "Update/Publish" and select "Save Draft" if you're not ready to go live.

2 Preview

Click the eye icon to see how your page looks to visitors.

3 Publish

Click "Publish" (or "Update" for existing pages) to make your page live.
