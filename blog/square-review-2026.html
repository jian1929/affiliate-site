What is Square?

Founded in 2009 by Twitter co-founder Jack Dorsey, Square started with a simple idea: make accepting card payments accessible to everyone. The original Square Reader—a small white dongle that plugged into a smartphone's headphone jack—democratized mobile payments when it launched.

Today, Square has evolved into a comprehensive commerce platform serving millions of businesses. The company (now Block, Inc.) processes over $100 billion in payments annually across its retail, restaurant, and online solutions.

Features Breakdown

Point of Sale (POS)

Free, full-featured POS app works on iPad, Android tablet, and Square Stand. Includes inventory management, customer profiles, and real-time sales analytics.

Online Store

Build a professional e-commerce website with Square Online. Includes SEO tools, domain mapping, and integrated checkout. Free plan available.

Invoices & Estimates

Create and send professional invoices. Customers can pay online via link. Includes automatic reminders and recurring billing.

Next-day deposits standard. Instant deposits available for a 1% fee. Supports all major credit cards and digital wallets.

Conclusion

Square remains one of the best payment processing options for small to medium businesses, particularly those with physical retail or restaurant operations. Its no-monthly-fee model removes barriers to entry, while the all-in-one platform handles everything from point of sale to inventory management.

The 2.6% + 10¢ rate is competitive for the feature set included, especially when you factor in the free POS software, inventory tools, and customer management. For high-volume businesses processing $50,000+ monthly, custom pricing may be worth negotiating.

If your business is primarily online or you need the most developer-friendly API, Stripe remains the better choice. But for omnichannel businesses that sell both in-person and online, Square's unified platform and excellent hardware make it a top recommendation.
