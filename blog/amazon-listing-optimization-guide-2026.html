Keyword Research for Amazon

Keywords are the foundation of your listing. Every optimization decision—from titles to bullets to backend terms—starts with understanding what your customers search for.

Primary vs. Secondary Keywords

Primary keywords directly describe your product (e.g., "wireless headphones"). Secondary keywords describe use cases, complementary products, or variations (e.g., "gym headphones", "workout earbuds").

Keyword Research Process

  1. Brainstorm seed keywords — List 10-15 words describing your product
  2. Use Helium 10's Cerebro or Magnet — Find thousands of related keywords with search volume and competition data
  3. Check Amazon auto-suggest — Type in search bar to see real buyer queries
  4. Analyze competitor listings — See what keywords top sellers use in titles and bullets
  5. Review search terms report — Once live, see which keywords actually convert

Keyword Organization

Categorize your keywords into three tiers:

  • Tier 1 (Head Terms) — High volume, high competition (e.g., "laptop stand")
  • Tier 2 (Body Terms) — Medium volume, medium competition (e.g., "adjustable laptop stand")
  • Tier 3 (Long Tail) — Low volume, low competition (e.g., "ergonomic laptop stand for home office")

The Perfect Title Formula

Amazon titles have a 200-character limit (200 bytes, technically), but the first 80 characters are most critical—they display on mobile and in search results before expansion.

Brand + Primary Keyword + Secondary Keyword(s) + Key Feature/Benefit + Size/Quantity + Color

Title Optimization Rules

  • Front-load keywords — Put most important terms first (before the fold)
  • Use proper capitalization — Title Case, not ALL CAPS
  • Include numbers — "6 Pack" outperforms "Six Pack"
  • Separate elements with pipes or commas — Improves readability
  • Don't stuff keywords — Read naturally; repetition hurts conversions
  • Include key differentiators — What makes yours different from competitors?

Title Example

Before: Laptop Stand Adjustable Aluminum Computer Stand Compatible with MacBook Pro Air Notebook Tablet Ergonomic Foldable Portable Silver

After: Adjustable Laptop Stand - Foldable Aluminum Computer Riser for MacBook Pro/Air, Notebook & Tablet - Ergonomic Desk Organizer with Cooling Vent - Silver

⚠️ Amazon Title Requirements: Don't use promotional phrases ("Best Seller", "Sale"), HTML markup, or special characters (©, ®, ™). These can result in listing suppression.

Converting Bullet Points

Amazon allows 5 bullet points with 500 characters each. These are your conversion powerhouse—they address objections, highlight benefits, and answer questions before buyers ask.

The A+ Content Formula for Bullets

  1. Feature — What the product does
  2. Benefit — Why it matters to the customer
  3. Proof point — Specific details that validate the claim

Bullet Point Templates

Benefit-First (Emotional):

"Protect your laptop from spills and scratches with our premium neoprene sleeve—soft interior lining prevents scratches while the water-resistant exterior keeps your device dry in any weather."

Problem-Solution:

"Tired of tangled cables? Our magnetic charging cable set includes 3 premium cables with 360° rotation connectors that snap together magnetically—no more mess, no more frustration."

Specification-First (Logical):

"Built to last: Aircraft-grade aluminum construction supports up to 25 lbs, adjustable height from 6" to 10", fits laptops 10-15.6" with non-slip silicone pads."

What to Include in Each Bullet

  • Primary benefit of the feature
  • Specific measurements or quantities
  • Materials or quality indicators
  • Compatibility information
  • Usage instructions or tips
  • Warranty or guarantee details

High-Converting Images

Images account for 80% of above-the-fold content on mobile. Buyers make snap judgments based on visuals. Every image must earn its place.

The 7 Essential Images

  1. Main Image (Hero) — White background, product fills 85% of frame, no props
  2. Infographic — Key features and benefits in visual format
  3. Lifestyle — Product in use context (kitchen counter, wearing it, etc.)
  4. Feature Close-Up — Detail shot of most important feature
  5. Size/Scale — Shows true dimensions with ruler or human model
  6. Comparison (optional) — Before/after or vs. competitors
  7. Instructional — How to use, assembly, or care instructions

Image Specifications

  • Minimum: 1000 x 1000 pixels (enables zoom)
  • Recommended: 2560 x 2560 pixels
  • Format: JPEG or PNG
  • Color mode: RGB

Backend Keywords (Search Terms)

Hidden from shoppers but read by Amazon's algorithm, backend search terms are your opportunity to include keywords you couldn't fit in the visible content.

Backend Search Terms Best Practices

  • 249-byte limit — Approximately 250 characters total
  • No duplicates — Don't repeat front-end keywords
  • No competitor names — This violates Amazon's policies
  • Use singular and plural — Amazon handles both, but doesn't hurt to include both
  • Include misspellings — Capture common typos buyers use
  • Use spaces, not commas — Commas waste characters

Backend Keyword Strategy

Organize by priority:

  1. High-volume misspellings (most impactful)
  2. Alternate product names (what else buyers call it)
  3. Use case keywords (where/when they use it)
  4. Targeted long-tails (3-5 word phrases)

Pricing Strategy

Even the best-optimized listing fails if the price is wrong. Price communicates value—too high and buyers bounce, too low and you leave money on the table.

Competitive Price Analysis

  1. Check Buy Box price range — Where do most sales occur?
  2. Analyze review counts — New sellers typically need competitive pricing
  3. Calculate landed cost — Include shipping, FBA fees, and COGS
  4. Factor in PPC costs — Budget for advertising during launch

Landed Cost = Product Cost + Shipping + FBA Fees + PPC Cost
Minimum Price = Landed Cost + Minimum Profit Margin

Psychological Pricing

Small price differences dramatically affect perceived value:

  • $19.97 feels closer to $19 than $20 (left-digit effect)
  • $24.99 outperforms $25.00 despite only saving $0.01
  • Bundles priced at $XX.97 signal "deal" to bargain hunters

Optimization Checklist

Use this checklist to ensure your listing is fully optimized before launch:

Keywords

  • Primary keyword in title (within first 80 characters)
  • Secondary keywords distributed across title and bullets
  • Long-tail keywords in backend search terms
  • Misspellings captured in backend
  • No duplicate keywords

Title

  • Under 200 characters
  • Primary keyword front-loaded
  • Brand name included
  • Size/quantity clear
  • Key differentiator highlighted

Bullets

  • 5 bullets (all 500 characters utilized)
  • Each bullet follows feature-benefit-proof format
  • Measurements and quantities included
  • Warranty/guarantee mentioned
  • Reads naturally, not keyword-stuffed

Images

  • Main image on pure white background
  • Infographic highlights key benefits
  • Lifestyle image shows product in use
  • Size/scale reference included
  • All images minimum 1000x1000px

Price

  • Competitive with Buy Box range
  • Covers all costs with profit margin
  • Considered for PPC budget
  • Psychological pricing applied
