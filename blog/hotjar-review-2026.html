What is Hotjar?

Hotjar is a powerful user behavior analytics tool that helps you understand how users interact with your website through visual insights. Unlike traditional analytics tools that focus on numbers and metrics, Hotjar shows you exactly what users do through heatmaps, session recordings, and direct feedback collection.

Founded in 2014, Hotjar has become an essential tool for UX designers, product managers, marketers, and developers who want to see beyond analytics dashboards and understand the actual user experience. It's particularly valuable for identifying usability issues, understanding user behavior patterns, and gathering qualitative feedback.

For affiliate marketers, Hotjar provides unique insights that can dramatically improve conversion rates. By seeing exactly where users click, how far they scroll, and where they get confused, you can optimize your content layout, CTAs, and affiliate link placement for maximum effectiveness.

Key Takeaway: Hotjar answers the question "why?" that traditional analytics can't. While Google Analytics tells you what users do (they leave on this page), Hotjar shows you why (they can't find the CTA, it's buried below the fold). This qualitative insight is invaluable for conversion optimization.

Key Features for Understanding Users

Heatmaps

Heatmaps visualize aggregate user behavior in a visual overlay on your pages. Hotjar offers four types of heatmaps:

  • Click maps - Show where users click, tap, and scroll. Reveal which elements attract attention and interaction.
  • Move maps - Track mouse movement (on desktop) to understand visual attention patterns.
  • Scroll maps - Show how far users scroll on each page. Identify the "fold" and where engagement drops off.
  • Content engagement - Combine scroll and interaction data to show engagement with specific content sections.

Heatmaps are invaluable for understanding which parts of your affiliate content users actually see and interact with.

Session Recordings

Watch recordings of actual user sessions to see exactly how people interact with your site. You'll see mouse movements, clicks, scrolls, and navigation patterns. Session recordings help you:

  • Identify frustrating UX issues like broken links or confusing navigation
  • Understand rage clicks (repeated clicks on non-clickable elements)
  • Spot dead clicks and unreachable content
  • See how users actually find and use your content
  • Discover unexpected user behaviors and paths

For affiliate sites, recordings reveal whether users are finding your product recommendations and CTAs.

Incoming Feedback and Surveys

Collect qualitative feedback directly from users with Hotjar's survey tools:

  • Targeted surveys - Trigger surveys based on user behavior (e.g., after visiting a product page)
  • Exit-intent surveys - Capture feedback from users about to leave
  • NPS and CSAT - Measure customer satisfaction and loyalty
  • Custom questions - Ask anything relevant to your optimization goals

Surveys help you understand the "why" behind user behavior and get direct input on what improvements matter most.

Visual Feedback Widget

Hotjar's feedback widget allows users to share thoughts without disrupting their experience. Users can click a button to leave feedback, annotate screenshots, and provide context that traditional analytics misses. This creates a continuous feedback loop that helps you understand user sentiment in real-time.

Advanced Features

Beyond core features, Hotjar offers powerful capabilities for serious optimization:

  • Funnels - Track drop-off through custom funnels with recording access at each step
  • Conversion Funnels - Identify where users leave and watch their recordings to understand why
  • Form Analysis - See how users interact with forms, where they hesitate, and where they abandon
  • Automations - Trigger actions based on user behavior (e.g., show a survey after high engagement)
  • Integrations - Connect with Slack, HubSpot, Zapier, and other tools for workflow automation

Getting Started with Hotjar

Setting up Hotjar takes just minutes, but proper configuration ensures you get the most valuable insights:

1. Install the Tracking Code

Add Hotjar's tracking code to your website. For most sites, this is a simple copy-paste into your site's header (before the closing tag). Hotjar supports all major platforms including WordPress, Shopify, Webflow, and custom sites. You can also use Google Tag Manager for easier management.

2. Configure Tracking Settings

Set up what you want to track. Configure session sampling (track all sessions or a percentage), enable form and link tracking, set up IP anonymization for privacy compliance, and configure which pages to track. Proper configuration ensures you capture the data that matters without overwhelming your recording limits.

3. Create Your First Heatmap

Start by creating heatmaps for your most important pages: homepage, top landing pages, product review pages, and key conversion pages. Set the heatmap to accumulate data for at least a few days to get statistically significant results. Review the heatmap weekly to identify patterns and optimization opportunities.

4. Review Session Recordings

Watch recordings to understand actual user behavior. Start with users who completed valuable actions (converted, signed up) and those who didn't. Look for patterns in frustration signals (rage clicks, confusion, abandonment) and note what successful users do differently.

5. Set Up Targeted Surveys

Create surveys to collect direct feedback. For affiliate sites, consider surveys asking about content helpfulness, product recommendation clarity, and CTA visibility. Use behavior-triggered surveys to capture feedback at relevant moments.

Hotjar for Affiliate Marketers

Hotjar provides unique insights that can directly improve your affiliate revenue:

Optimize Content Placement

Use scroll maps to see where the fold actually is on your pages. Many affiliate marketers place CTAs "above the fold" but scroll data often reveals users don't see that area. Heatmaps show exactly where users actually engage, helping you place affiliate links and CTAs where they'll be seen.

Test CTA Placement

Create heatmaps for pages with affiliate CTAs. See if users are actually clicking where you want them to. If your "Buy Now" button gets few clicks but users are clicking elsewhere, you need to adjust placement or design.

Identify Confusion Points

Watch session recordings for users visiting product comparison pages. Look for rage clicks (indicating something should be clickable but isn't), confusion patterns, and abandonment. This feedback directly informs content improvements.

Improve Product Recommendations

Use surveys to ask users about your product recommendations. "Was this review helpful?" and "Did you find what you were looking for?" provide direct feedback on content quality and relevance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hotjar free?

Yes, Hotjar offers a free plan with 35 daily sessions, 1 heatmap, basic recordings, and 1 survey. This is excellent for small websites and testing the tool. Paid plans start at $32/month for additional features and recording volume.

How does Hotjar compare to Microsoft Clarity?

Microsoft Clarity is completely free with unlimited heatmaps and recordings, making it attractive for basic needs. However, Hotjar offers more comprehensive features including surveys, feedback widgets, advanced filtering, and better visualization tools. For serious optimization, Hotjar's additional features justify the cost.

Does Hotjar track mobile users?

Hotjar tracks mobile sessions, but capabilities are more limited than desktop. Click tracking and basic engagement data are available, but mouse movement tracking doesn't apply. For mobile-focused sites, consider combining Hotjar with other analytics tools that provide more detailed mobile insights.

Is Hotjar privacy-compliant?

Yes, Hotjar is GDPR, CCPA, and PECR compliant. It includes IP anonymization, cookie consent handling, data retention controls, and options to exclude specific users or roles. You can also configure automatic data deletion and honor Do Not Track browser settings.

How many sessions do I need?

It depends on your traffic and goals. For heatmaps, more data is generally better (aim for at least 100-200 visits per page). For recordings, review 20-50 sessions per segment to identify patterns. Start with the free plan to understand your needs, then upgrade when you hit limits.
