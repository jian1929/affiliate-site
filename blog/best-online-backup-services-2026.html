What to Look for in Online Backup

Unlimited vs. Capped Storage

Unlimited backup plans (Backblaze, Carbonite) mean you never worry about storage limits. Capped plans (IDrive, Acronis) offer more storage but with limits. For most users, unlimited is the better value.

Restore Options

Check restore options carefully: cloud restore (download), physical drive restore (mail), and local network restore. Businesses should prioritize fast restore options and disaster recovery capabilities.

Versioning

Versioning lets you restore previous versions of files - crucial for recovering from ransomware or accidental changes. Most services offer 30 days; some offer unlimited versioning.

Security Features

Look for end-to-end encryption (zero-knowledge preferred), two-factor authentication, and ransomware protection. Acronis leads in security; Backblaze offers solid encryption at no extra cost.

Upload Speed

Initial backup can take days for large datasets. Some services offer "seed" restore (mailing a hard drive) for faster initial backup. Consider this for multi-TB backups.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is online backup really necessary?

Absolutely. Data loss statistics show 29% of data loss is caused by accidental deletion, 43% by hardware failure, and 13% by malware/ransomware. Cloud backup is your insurance against all of these scenarios.

How much storage do I need?

The average computer has 500GB-1TB of data. For most users, unlimited backup plans are the best value since you don't have to monitor storage usage. Power users with large media libraries should consider plans with 2TB+.

How long does initial backup take?

It depends on your internet speed and data volume. A 500GB backup on a 50Mbps connection takes roughly 22 hours. Use services with "seed" restore (mail a hard drive) for faster initial backup of large datasets.

Is my data secure with cloud backup?

Yes, reputable services use 256-bit AES encryption in transit and at rest. For maximum privacy, use services with zero-knowledge encryption where only you have the decryption key.

What's the difference between backup and sync?

Backup creates historical versions and protects against data loss. Sync (like Dropbox or Google Drive) keeps files current across devices but doesn't protect against accidental deletion or ransomware. Use both for complete protection.
