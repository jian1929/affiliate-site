Pricing Plans (2026)

Slack offers four distinct plans designed to serve everyone from small teams to large enterprises. Understanding the differences is crucial for choosing the right tier.

Plan Price Key Features
Free Free (forever) 90-day message history, 10 integrations, 1:1 calls
Pro $8.75/user/month Unlimited message history, unlimited integrations, group calls
Business+ $15/user/month Compliance exports, SAML SSO, 24/7 support
Enterprise Grid Custom pricing Enterprise security, unlimited workspaces, advanced admin

Prices shown are per-user, per-month when billed annually. Slack also offers a 25% discount for nonprofits and 90-day free trial for new teams.

Which Plan is Right for You?

Free Plan: Best for small teams or casual use. Limited to 90 days of message history can be a significant constraint for growing organizations, but it's a great way to evaluate Slack before committing.

Pro Plan: The sweet spot for most businesses. Unlimited message history is essential for knowledge retention and onboarding new team members. This is the recommended starting point for serious teams.

Business+ Plan: Ideal for organizations with compliance requirements or those needing advanced security features. SAML SSO integration is crucial for larger enterprises.

Enterprise Grid: Built for massive organizations with complex security, compliance, and administrative needs. Contact sales for custom pricing based on your requirements.

Key Features

Channels

Channels are the backbone of Slack's organizational structure. They allow teams to create dedicated spaces for specific topics, projects, departments, or any purpose imaginable. You can create public channels for company-wide announcements or private channels for sensitive discussions. The ability to organize channels with prefixes (like #marketing-campaigns or #engineering-backend) keeps everything searchable and manageable.

Threads

Threading keeps conversations organized by allowing replies to specific messages without cluttering the main channel. This feature is invaluable for discussions that would otherwise create notification overload. Team members can follow threads they're interested in while maintaining a clean channel experience.

Slack Huddles

Introduced as a lightweight alternative to traditional video calls, Slack Huddles allow spontaneous audio and screen sharing conversations. They're perfect for quick discussions, pair programming, or just casual team check-ins. Huddles can be started instantly from any channel or direct message, reducing the friction of coordinating meetings.

Slack Clips

Slack Clips let you record and share video, audio, or screen recordings. This asynchronous communication tool is particularly valuable for remote teams spread across time zones or teams that want to share detailed explanations without scheduling a meeting. Clips can be annotated with timestamps and reactions, making feedback collection seamless.

Powerful Search

Slack's search functionality is one of its strongest features. You can search across all messages, files, and content with advanced filters for date ranges, specific channels, people, and file types. Saved searches let you bookmark frequently needed queries. The search capability is what makes Slack's message history retention so valuable.

Automation with Slack Workflows

Slack Workflows allows teams to automate routine tasks without code. You can create workflows for common processes like onboarding, request forms, approval chains, or any business process. The visual workflow builder makes it accessible to non-technical users while providing enough power for complex automations.

Slack Canvas

Canvases are collaborative documents embedded directly in Slack. They combine the best of wikis, documents, and project management into a single space. Teams use Canvases for meeting notes, project briefs, team handbooks, and any content that needs to live alongside your communication.

Integrations and Ecosystem

Slack's true power comes from its integration ecosystem. With over 2,600 apps in the Slack App Directory, there's likely a pre-built integration for your needs. Here are the most valuable categories:

Productivity Tools

  • Google Workspace: Receive calendar invites, sync Drive files, search Gmail
  • Microsoft 365: Sync with Outlook, Teams meetings, OneDrive files
  • Notion: Get updates, share pages, create entries from Slack

Development Tools

  • GitHub: Get repository updates, PR notifications, deployment status
  • Jira: Create issues, receive updates, search across projects
  • PagerDuty: Alert on-call teams, acknowledge incidents

Communication and Marketing

  • Salesforce: Get CRM updates, share opportunities, sync contacts
  • HubSpot: Lead notifications, deal updates, customer activity
  • Mailchimp: Campaign performance, subscriber activity

Custom Integrations

Beyond pre-built apps, Slack offers robust API access for custom integrations. Whether you need to connect internal tools, automate workflows, or build custom applications, Slack's developer platform provides the flexibility to make it happen.

Final Verdict

Is Slack Right for Your Team?

Slack has earned its position as the default choice for modern workplace communication. Its combination of intuitive design, powerful features, and unmatched integration ecosystem makes it the go-to solution for teams ranging from startups to Fortune 500 enterprises.

Choose Slack if:

  • You need seamless integration with your existing tools
  • Your team values asynchronous communication with strong search
  • Remote or hybrid work is part of your setup
  • You want a platform that team members will actually enjoy using
  • Organization-wide visibility into team communication matters
  • You need robust mobile support for on-the-go teams

  • Budget is a primary constraint (check out our comparison of alternatives)
  • You primarily use Microsoft products (Teams might integrate better)
  • You need native heavy video conferencing features

For most organizations, Slack represents the gold standard of team communication. The investment in a paid plan is justified by the productivity gains, reduced email overload, and improved team cohesion. If you're evaluating team communication tools, Slack should be at the top of your list.
