What Is WooCommerce?

WooCommerce is the world's most popular eCommerce platform, powering over 4 million active online stores. Originally launched in 2011 as a WordPress plugin, it has since grown to become the de facto standard for building online stores on the WordPress platform.

Unlike hosted solutions, WooCommerce is open-source software that you install on your own WordPress website. This gives you complete ownership and control over your store, data, and customer relationships. There are no monthly platform fees for the core software, making it an attractive option for businesses of all sizes.

Why Millions Choose WooCommerce

WooCommerce combines the flexibility of WordPress with powerful eCommerce capabilities. You get access to thousands of themes and extensions, full ownership of your data, and the ability to customize every aspect of your store. Whether you're selling physical products, digital downloads, subscriptions, or services, WooCommerce has you covered.

Core Features That Matter

Product Management

Upload unlimited products with variations, attributes, and custom fields. Support for physical, digital, and affiliate products.

Payment Gateways

Built-in support for PayPal, Stripe, and credit cards. Connect to 100+ payment processors worldwide.

Shipping Solutions

Flexible shipping zones, live rates from carriers, flat rate options, and free shipping configurations.

Inventory Management

Track stock levels, low stock alerts, backorder handling, and inventory reports built-in.

Tax Configuration

Automatic tax calculations, EU VAT support, tax rates by location, and reporting tools.

Account Management

Customer accounts, order history, wishlists, and saved payment methods for repeat purchases.

WooCommerce vs The Competition

WooCommerce vs Shopify

Winner: Depends on your needs

  • WooCommerce = More control, no monthly fees, full data ownership
  • Shopify = Easier setup, managed hosting, all-in-one solution

Choose WooCommerce if you want flexibility and don't mind managing your own hosting. Choose Shopify if you want simplicity and don't need as much customization.

WooCommerce vs BigCommerce

Winner: WooCommerce for control, BigCommerce for simplicity

  • WooCommerce = Open source, infinite customization, you own everything
  • BigCommerce = Built-in features, less maintenance, some SaaS limitations

WooCommerce gives you complete control but requires more technical knowledge. BigCommerce offers more out-of-the-box features with less maintenance.
